10.03 Review Solutions

The answers to the review for Module 9 are given below. Please contact your instructor if you have questions about the problems that you miss.

Solve and graph inequalities

  1. x > 6; the graph has an open dot on the 6, and an arrow to the right

    x > 1; the graph has a closed dot on the 1, and an arrow pointing right.

    4 < x < -5; this cannot happen so there is no real solution. There is no graph.

    4 > x > -5; there is an open dot on 4, an open dot on -5 and a line between those points.

    x < 4 or x > -5; this includes all real numbers, which is the correct answer. The graph is a line with arrows on both ends, stopping at no point.

  2. x > 4 or x < -5; there is an open dot on the 4 with an arrow to the right, and a closed dot on the -5 with an arrow pointing left.

Systems of Inequalities

  1. y > 4x + 1
    y < -2x + 3

    Any point in the shaded area is an answer to the systems. The point (0,0) will work.


  2. y < -x + 3
    y > x - 4

    The track point represents one of an infinity of correct answers.
