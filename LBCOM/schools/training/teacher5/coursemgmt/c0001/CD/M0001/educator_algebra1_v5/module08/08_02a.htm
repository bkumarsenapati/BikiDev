08.02 Multiplying and Dividing Rational Expressions

Now that you know how to simplify, you can do other math operations with rational expressions. Remember you are mastering all of these skills so you can solve more complex equations and prepare for upper level math.

In this lesson, you will learn how to multiply and divide rational expressions. Keep in mind that rational expressions have the same properties as fractions. Rational expressions are fractions with variables in them.


In an effort to prepare your mind for working with rational expressions, take a look at some multiplication and division of fractions. As we mentioned in the previous lesson, at this point in your math career, you may have a good handle on multiplying and dividing fractions so the process below may seem tedious. However, be sure to read through it anyway because the "thought process" will lead you to a better understanding of how to multiply and divide rational expressions.

There are many ways to multiply fractions. Some of you may multiply and then reduce. Some of you may cross cancel and then multiply. Please do your best to come to an understanding of the method shown below. It is a correct method and similar to how we will work with the rational expressions.

Division of fractions

To divide fractions, you must first change the problem to multiplication and flip the second fraction. This is called multiplying by the reciprocal. Multiply the numerators and multiply the denominators. Let's summarize.

  1. If you have a division problem, change it to multiplication and flip the second fraction. Multiply the numerators and the denominators. Because you want to cancel out the common factors, it is best not to actually multiply the numbers but instead to just write them together. Factor the numerator and denominator completely. Cancel the common factors.
  2. The final answer consists of the remaining factors.
