06.02 Multiplication and Division of Polys

Now that you are an expert with addition and subtraction of polynomials, we can add a new skill to your bag of tricks. This time let's work on multiplication and division.

Be Careful! Later these skills will get mixed up a bit. Keep a running list of the rules in your notes.


In this lesson, we will concentrate on multiplication and division of monomials.

Multiplication of monomials

Before we give a rule for multiplication, it is a good idea to look at the concept. This will build a foundation for your understanding of the shortcut.


Example 1:
( x3 )( x5 ) x to the third means you have three factors of x, x to the fifth means you have five factors of x
 
x8 You have a total of 8 factors of x

Example 2:
( 3x2 )( -2x4 ) Multiply the 3 and the -2 and then the x squared and the x to the fourth
 
-6x6 This gives you negative 6 with the 3 and negative 2 and x to the sixth with the total of 6 x's.

Shortcut:

Multiply the coefficients and add the exponents.

Division of monomials

Again, before we give a rule it is best to look at the concept of division.This will build a foundation for your understanding of the shortcut.


Example 1:
There are 5 factors of x in the numerator and there are 2 factors of x in the denominator.

Since x divided by x is equal to 1, you can cancel out the common factors in both the numerator and denominator.

There are three factors of x left.

Example 2:
There are 2 factors of a and 1 factor of b in the numerator and 1 factor of a and 3 factors of b in the denominator.

 

You can now cancel out the common factors including the threes.

There is 1 factor of a left in the numerator and 2 factors of b left in the denominator.


Shortcut:

Divide the numbers and subtract the exponents
