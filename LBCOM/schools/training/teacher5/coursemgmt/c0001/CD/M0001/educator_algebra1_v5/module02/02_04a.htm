02.04 Problems

This assignment shows how we use graphs to interpret data. Take your time and read the questions carefully.


Bar Graph Example
What's the Story?

The data in the bar graph to the right is actual temperature data gathered from the Internet.


Bar Graph Questions

  1. Which month (or months) have the highest value and which has the lowest? What are those values?
  2. The x-axis on the bar graph above should be labeled months of the year. What would be an appropriate label for the y-axis?
  3. What trends do you notice in the data?
  4. Create a title for the bar graph.
  5. Could a line graph be used to show this data? Why or why not?

Chart Example
Vacation time!

January is a great time to take a trip to Crystal River to snorkel with the manatees! While the air temperature in January is often cool, the temperature of the water in the natural springs stays constant (about 68 degrees) year round. (Although you may still like to wear a wet suit!)

As gentle as this magnificent creature is, the manatee is struggling for survival. As part of the effort in saving this endangered species, the Florida Marine Research Institute has been tracking manatee mortality rates. The spreadsheet below contains data from January 1998 through August 1998.


Chart Questions

  1. Which month had the fewest number of deaths and which month had the greatest?

  2. What are the three main causes of death for manatees?

  3. Explain your theory for the decrease in the total number of deaths in August.

  4. What is your prediction for the total number of deaths in September? What is the basis for your prediction?
