01.03 Pace

There are three essential items for your trip through this Algebra world.

  1. Be consistent. Have a schedule and stick to it. Math is best learned when you work daily. You cannot do math in "chunks" so be sure NOT to procrastinate your work for this course.

  2. Be a good communicator. Call or email your instructor when you need help. Feedback from your instructor is vital to your success.

  3. Have a solid foundation. Be sure you are academically ready for this course. There will be a diagnostic test at the end of this module.

Pace

Think about the following questions: When would you like to be done with the course? Do you need the course to graduate? Are you a senior? How much time per day or week will you have to devote to the course?

There are many factors that will influence your decision. You need to talk to your parents, guidance counselor and instructor to determine which pace would be best for you.

The pace charts for your class can be found in the Course Information area. Print your pace chart. Put a copy in your notebook and your work area. Give a copy to your parent, guardian, or lab facilitator.

There may be several options. The accelerated, traditional, and extended pace options are described below.

Pace Here's an approximation of what you can expect at this pace:

Traditional Pace (recommended)
Complete the course in nine months

  • You will need to spend approximately 1.5-2 hours per day on the course.
  • You will need to complete approximately 3-5 assignments per week.
Accelerated Pace
Complete the course in seven months
  • You will need to spend approximately 2-2.5 hours per day on the course.
  • You will need to complete approximately 5-7 assignments per week.
Extended Pace
Requires teacher, parent, and guidance counselor approval.
  • Your individual pace will be designed by your teacher.
  • You will be required to submit a petition for extended pace documenting approval from your parent or guardian and your school guidance counselor.
  • More information about extended pace is provided in the course information area.
