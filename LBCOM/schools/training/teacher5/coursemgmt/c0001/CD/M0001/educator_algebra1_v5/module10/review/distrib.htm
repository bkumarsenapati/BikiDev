The Distributive Property

To solve equations you must learn to distribute properly. This is not a difficult procedure, but you must be very good at the details. Pay special attention to what happens when there is a negative sign in front of the parentheses.

  1. Removing parentheses when expressions are being subtracted.
    Example: (5a - 7b) - (2a - 3b) In this case we must understand that the "-"between the parentheses can represent a "-1." Rewrite the problem as: (5a - 7b) -1(2a - 3b). Now distribute (multiply) to both terms in the parentheses following the -1, and remove parentheses at the same time. -1(2a) is -2a and -1(-3b) is +3b. Watch what happens to the signs. 5a - 7b - 2a + 3b. Now we are ready to collect like terms, the same way we did in Example 1 by adding their coefficients.
    and we get 3a - 4b.

    Just two more.

  2. Example: (2a - 7) +3( 5a - 1) First distribute (multiply) and remove parentheses to get
    2a - 7 + 15a - 3. Here the signs do not change because we are multiplying by a positive number. Collect like terms to get 17a - 10. Now you know what like terms are, do you still need the colors?

  3. Example: (6x - 2y) -4(-3x +4y) First distribute (multiply) the "-4" to both terms in the parentheses following the "-4" and remove parentheses to get 6x - 2y +12x - 16y. Do you understand what happened with the signs? The changes are a result of multiplying by a negative number. Now collect like terms to get 18x - 18y.

Now you are ready to finish your review by working the five problems below. Don't forget to collect like terms after distributing.
1. 15m - ( 5n - 8m)
2. 3(x - 3) + 18
3. -4(x + 1) + 2
4. 3(m - 5) + 1 - 2(m + 2)
5. 3(y - 7) - (9y - 1) + 10
