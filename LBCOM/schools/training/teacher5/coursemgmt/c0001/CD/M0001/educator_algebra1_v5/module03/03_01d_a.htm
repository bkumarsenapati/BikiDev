03.01 Vocabulary: Graphic Organizers

Graphic organizers are a pictorial way of representing groups or categories and their relationships. These are used in math for grouping number types, chemistry for grouping elements and compounds, and in biology for grouping plants and animals. This method can be used for many things. Let's look at something you all understand, your wardrobe.

If you want to group your shirts, you might start by classifying them by collar. We'll form two groups then: collars and no collars.

Some of your collared shirts might be polos and some dress shirts. If you have tank tops, none of them have collars.

  2. The circles for collared and no collared shirts do not touch because there are no shirts that have both characteristics.
  3. Polos and dress shirts both have collars so they are entirely in the collared circle. There are some shirts that are both polos and dress shirts. That's why those two circles have a part in common. The green part represents dress shirts that are also polos.
  4. All tank tops are shirts without collars, so that circle is entirely within the no collared circle.
