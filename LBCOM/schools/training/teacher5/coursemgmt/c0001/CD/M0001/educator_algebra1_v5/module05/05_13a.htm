05.13 Substitution Method

The last method you will learn for solving systems of equations is called the Substitution Method.

Look at the following system and think of all the ways you can solve this.

a) 2x + y = 11
b) 4x - 3y = 7

Check your thoughts

Look at these equations and see if it would be easy to solve for one of the variables in terms of the other. It seems that we could solve equation 'a' for y without a lot of hassle.

a) 2x + y = 11
b) 4x - 3y = 7

The objective of the Substitution method is to solve for one of the variables and substitute the expression into the other equation. Let's try this.

Step 1: Solve for one of the variables in one of the equations. Solve for the variable that has a coefficient of 1. You will usually avoid fractions this way. In our system of equations above, we will solve for the y in the first equation.

Solve 2x + y = 11 for y [add -2x to each side of the equation]
           y = -2x + 11  

We now know y but it is in terms of x. That's okay..watch how this works.

Step 2: Substitute the value of y into the other equation.

4x - 3 y = 7       [substitute -2x + 11 for y]
4x - 3 (-2x + 11) = 7  

 

Step 3 : Solve the equation to find the value of x. (Do you see why learning to solve equations is so important?)

4x - 3(-2x + 11) = 7   [distribute the -3 first]
4x + 6x - 33 = 7 [combine like terms]
10x - 33 = 7 [add 33 to each side of the equation]
10x = 7 + 33  
10x = 40 [divide by 10 to solve for x]
x = 4  

Step 4 : Substitute your x value into one of the original equations.

We have the value of x but we need the value of y in order to have the point at which the lines would intersect. Remember we are looking for an ordered pair solution.

To find the value of y, Let's use 2x + y = 11. We know that x = 4 so here is the work for finding the value of y.

2x + y = 11
2(4) + y = 11
8 + y = 11
y = 11 - 8
y = 3

The solution to this system of equations is the point (4, 3) .

This is the point at which the two lines would intersect. This point will work in both equations.

Let's do one more

Let's solve the following system of equations:

Equation 1: x + y = 4
Equation 2: -2x + y = 1

Think of all your options:

You could...
1. Graph the lines and find the point of intersection
2. Subtract equation 2 from equation 1 and use the addition method
3. Multiply equation 1 by 2 and use the addition method
4. Solve equation 1 or 2 for y and use the substitution method
5. Solve equation 1 for x and use the substitution method

We will use the Substitution Method to solve this system.

Step 1: Solve for one of the variables in one of the equations. Solve for the variable that has a coefficient of 1. You will avoid fractions if you do this. In our system of equations above, we have a lot of choices because most of the variables have a coefficient of 1. Let's solve for x in the first equation.

x + y = 4 [subtract y from both sides of the equation]
x = 4 - y  

Step 2: Substitute the value of x into the second equation of the system.

-2 x + y = 1 [substitute 4 - y for x]
-2 (4 - y) + y = 1  

Step 3: Solve the equation to find the value of y.

-2(4 - y) + y = 1 [do not forget to distribute the -2 first]
-8 + 2y + y = 1 [combine like terms]
-8 + 3y = 1 [add 8 to both sides of the equation]
3y = 1 + 8  
3y = 9 [divide by 3 to solve for y]
y = 3  

Step 4: Substitute y = 3 into one of the original equations

x + y = 4 [substitute 3 for y]
x + 3 = 4  
x = 4 - 3 [add -3 to both sides of the equation]
x = 1  


The solution to this system of equations is the point (1, 3) .

This is the point where the two lines intersect. This ordered pair solves both systems.
