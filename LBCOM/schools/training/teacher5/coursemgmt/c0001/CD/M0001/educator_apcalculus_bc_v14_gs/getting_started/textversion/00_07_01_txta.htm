Florida Virtual School Welcomes you to Understanding Academic Honesty
A Quick Start Tutorial
with a little help from Honest Abe

Academic Honesty means this: work you submit is created by you and is an original representation of your work.
The Work You Submit is Yours.
[Abraham Lincoln]

DO your own assignment research.
DO NOT copy, borrow or steal work of others or from the Internet.

Why?

Because you must earn credit honestly and demonstrate Mastery on your course's content.

FLVS no-no's: Plagiarism and Cheating

Plagiarism: Deliberate copy of another's work as your own or not citing authorship properly.

Cheating: Rule breaking, dishonesty or giving/receiving work from another student.

For example,

If you're taking a foreign language, your teacher wants to assess what you have learned in that language,

not what can be copied and pasted from a translation website.
[Bonjour!]

Your teacher uses Proctored Exams, Online Comparison Tools and Discussion-Based Assessments to check your work.

Taking an online course or exam on behalf of another person is a violation that could result in a 2nd DEGREE MISDEMEANOR and is punishable by law!
So do the right thing: DON'T CHEAT!
Florida Digital Learning Statute 1002.321

Help us help you! Always do your own work!
