Rationalizing Radical Expressions

Occasionally, you may end up with a square root on the denominator of your simplified fraction. This is not allowed in the world of Algebra. If you find yourself in this predicament, you simply have to rationalize.

If you have a square root in the denominator that is irrational, you rationalize. Irrational square roots are expressions like the square root of 8, the square root of 15, or the square root of 2. Let me repeat, you will ONLY do this step if you have a square root in the denominator that cannot be simplified.

You will "rationalize" by multiplying the numerator and denominator of the expression by the irrational square root. Take a look at the problem below to see how this process works.
