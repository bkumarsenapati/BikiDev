Falling Down the Rabbit Hole!

In this lesson you will learn how a containing shape will become arbitrarily small as n becomes large—and you get to use the clever sigma notation to show it!

Lesson Objectives

After completing this lesson, you will be able to:

  • understand Integrals with Archimedes’ Method of Exhaustion (numerical approximation)
  • understand how Archimedes’ Method of Exhaustion leads to the natural use of the rectangle approximation method for the area under a curve
  • represent the area under a curve as a limit using sigma notation
  • sketch the curve of a function based upon information from its first and second derivatives and vice versa

To meet these objectives you will review the following instructional videos:

  • Archimedes Area Approximation
  • The Rectangular Approximation Method for Area
  • Riemann Sum Examples
