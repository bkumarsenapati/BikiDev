07.01 GCF

In the previous module, you learned all about polynomials including how to add, subtract, multiply, divide and raise them to powers.

Now, it is critical that we learn how to undo the multiplication process on polynomials. This process is called factoring, or breaking a polynomial into its factors.

Factoring out the Greatest Common Factor (Step 1 in the factoring process above.)

Remember when you first learned to distribute? A problem like 4(x + 2) would be come 4x + 8 because you multiplied the 4 times the x and the 4 times the 2.

Factoring out the Greatest Common Factor, GCF, is the opposite of this process.

Suppose you were given the binomial 4x + 8 and asked to factor out the GCF. You would look at the terms in this problem and determine the largest value that would divide into the 4x and the 8 with no remainder. In this case, the number 4 is the largest value that is a factor of both the 4x and the 8.

So, 4x + 8 = 4(x + 2).

4(x+2) is the factored answer. Notice the x and the 2 came from dividing the 4x + 8 by the number 4.


 Example 1

Find the GCF and factor the expression 2x + 6.


2x + 6 =

The greatest common factor of 2 and 6 is 2.

Since the 6 has no variables, there will be no variables in the GCF value for this problem.

GCF = 2

Step 1: Identify the Greatest Common Factor for all the terms in the polynomial.

(Another way to think of it is what number will divide into both 2 and 6 evenly?)

 

Step 2: Factor out the GCF by dividing each term by the common factor.

 
2x + 6 = 2(x + 3) Step 3: Write the answer with the GCF multiplied times the result of the division problem in step 2.

Notice that if you were to distribute the 2 through the parenthesis, you would get the expression that you started with. 2(x + 3) is equal to 2x + 6. This is a great way to check to see that you factored correctly.

 Example 2

Find the GCF and factor the expression 10x3 + 15x2.


10x3 + 15x2 =

The greatest common factor of 10 and 15 is 5.

The variables x3 and x2 have an x2 as their common factor.

GCF = 5x2

Step 1: Identify the Greatest Common Factor for all the terms in the polynomial.

The GCF is the largest common factor for the coefficients combined with the largest common factor for the variables.

Step 2: Factor out the GCF by dividing each term by the common factor.

 
10x3 + 15x2  =  5x2(2x + 3) Step 3: Write the answer with the GCF multiplied times the result of the division problem in step 2.

 Example 3

Find the GCF and factor the expression 8x3 - 4x2 - 10x.


8x3 - 4x2 - 10x =

The greatest common factor of 8, -4 and -10 is 2.

The variables x3, x2 and x have an x as their common factor.

GCF = 2x

Step 1: Identify the Greatest Common Factor for all the terms in the polynomial.

The GCF is the largest common factor for the coefficients combined with the largest common factor for the variables.

Step 2: Factor out the GCF by dividing each term by the common factor.

 
8x3 - 4x2 - 10x = 2x(4x2 - 2x - 5) Step 3: Write the answer with the GCF multiplied times the result of the division problem in step 2.

 Example 4

Find the GCF and factor the expression -6x4y - 9x3y2 + 3x2y3.

This example is special because the first term is negative. When this happens, it is important that you factor out a negative along with your GCF. This will change the signs of all the terms in the problem. That is ok!


-6x4y - 9x3y2 + 3x2y3 =

The greatest common factor of -6, -9, and 3 is -3. (See red note above.)

The variables x4y, x3y2 and x2y3 have an x2y as their common factor. (In other words, each term contains at least an x2 and a single y.)

GCF = -3x2y

Step 1: Identify the Greatest Common Factor for all the terms in the polynomial.

The GCF is the largest common factor for the coefficients combined with the largest common factor for the variables.

Step 2: Factor out the GCF by dividing each term by the common factor.

 

-6x4y - 9x3y2 + 3x2y3

= -3x2y (2x2 + 3xy - y2)

Step 3: Write the answer with the GCF multiplied times the result of the division problem in step 2.

Special note: If an expression does not have a common factor, then it is called a "prime" expression. It is the same as a prime number. A prime number has only two factors, 1 and itself. Some prime numbers are 2, 3, 5, 7 and 13.

A example of a prime expression would be 5x + 7. The terms do not have any common factors or letters so you cannot factor it. The expression is prime.

2x2 + 3x - 9 would also be prime because the terms do not have any common factors.
