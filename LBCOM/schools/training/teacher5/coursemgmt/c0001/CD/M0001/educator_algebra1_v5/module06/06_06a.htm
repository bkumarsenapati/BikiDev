06.06 Roots

Exponents and Roots are so similar that mathematicians even say they are opposites. If you add four, the opposite is to subtract four. If you multiply by four, the opposite is to divide by four. Well, if you square a number, the opposite is to square root! Is square root the only type of root? Of course not! Since there are powers of three, four, five, and so on, so must there be cubed roots, fourth roots, fifth roots, and so on.

In the activities and problems in this module, not only will you see exponents at work, but also roots. Let's begin at the "roots"!


In this lesson you will:

  1. Understand the vocabulary associated with roots. Understand the meaning of and make a list of perfect squares.
  2. Simplifying irrational roots.

Vocabulary A radical is a root of a number,such as. This is read "square root of 5." The square root symbol is also called "the radical sign."Since the keyboard on your computer does not have a square root symbol, you can type "sqrt5" when you type your work.When we take the square root of a number, there are actually two answers. Let's use as an example. What number times itself will equal 9?
Did you think 3?
You are right because 3 x 3 = 9. This is the meaning of square root. Since 3 x 3 = 9, = 3.However, there is another answer to the square root of 9. It is -3 because -3 x -3 = 9.So really, the number 9 has two roots; 3 and -3.So how will we know which root to use? Do we list them both? Read on for the answer to these questions. A principle square root is the positive square root. The roots of 9 are 3 and -3. (3 x 3 = 9) and (-3 x -3 = 9). When you see (using the square root symbol) , you are being asked for the principle root of 9 which is positive 3 only. If you are being asked for both "roots" (positive and negative), you will see +. If you are being asked for just the negative answer, you will see -.

In short, you will always give the principle or positive square root unless the problem indicates otherwise.

Simplifying non-perfect squares Finding the square root of perfect squares is fairly simple but what about all the other real numbers? The square root of numbers like 2,3,5,6,7,8,10 etc. are a little more complex. These numbers are called irrational square roots. There are two ways to deal with irrational square roots. If you are dealing with a real life situation and you want to know an approximate value of the square root, simply use a calculator. For example,would be equal to approximately 3.74.

If you are wanting an absolute answer, then simplifying the square root is the best way to go. Simplifying is what you will be learning in this lesson. In short, NO CALCULATOR! At least not for now.

Explanation We know that the square root of 25 is equal to 5 but let's consider why.As shown on the left, 25 is equal to 5 times 5. 5 is the root of 25 because it takes 2 factors of 5 to make the 25.So, what rule can we get out of this simple problem? The rule is this:

Every 2 factors under the square root are equivalent to 1 factor outside the square root.

Simplifying radical expressions with variables Now that you understand the concept of square roots of non-perfect squares, we can apply our rule to the simplification of variables under the square root. Remember the rule: Every 2 factors under the square root are equivalent to 1 factor outside the square root.

There are only two types of variables that you will have to simplify. The variable will either have an even power or an odd power. Let's look at a problem and explanation for both.
