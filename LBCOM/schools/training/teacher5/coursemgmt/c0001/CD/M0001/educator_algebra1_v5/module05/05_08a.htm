Writing a Linear Equation in Standard Form

Until now, we have been writing our linear equations in slope-intercept form
(y=mx +b). Many times we are asked to give the equation of a line in Standard Form. Standard Form of a linear equation is ax + by = c, where a, b, and c are integers and a is positive.

For example:

5x + 4y = 20 is in standard form. a = 5, b = 4, and c = 20.

-3x -5y = -8 is not in standard form, because a = -3, and a must be positive.

Changing a Linear Equation from Slope-Intercept Form to Standard Form.

To change an equation from slope-intercept form to standard form, the x and y terms must be moved to left side of the equation and the constant term must be on the right side of the equation. All fractions must be cleared and the coefficient in front of x must be positive.

Try this using the equation changer tool to change y =x - 5 to standard form . But of course we want you to get practice manipulating algebraic formulas so use the tool to check your work .

Note: If you are having problems with the above tool, you may need to download the free Flash Player from Macromedia.

Here is an example of doing it without a tool:

Change y = x - 5 to standard form.

a) Move the x to the left side of the equation. [subtract x from each side]

y - x = -5

b) Simplify the equation and put it in ax + by = c format .

- x + y = -5

In standard form, you cannot have fraction coefficients. The coefficient on the x term cannot be negative. So we will multiply both sides of the equation by -3.

-3(- x + y) = -3 (-5) [Distribute the -3 over the parenthesis]

c) Simplify the equation.

2x - 3y = 15 Now the equation is in standard form.
