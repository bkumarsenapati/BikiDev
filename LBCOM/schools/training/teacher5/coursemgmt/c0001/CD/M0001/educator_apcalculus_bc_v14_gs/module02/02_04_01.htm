No Holes Allowed!

In this lesson you will learn what a continuous function is and what that looks like on a graph. You will also learn about the cool consequence of continuity … Intermediate Value Theorem!

Lesson Objectives

After completing this lesson, you will be able to:

  • use limits to describe the behavior of a function
  • use a graphing calculator to get numerical approximations for limits
  • understand the connection between limits and continuity
  • use a functionâ€™s continuity to evaluate its limit at a point
  • be able to determine when a function is continuous (or discontinuous)
  • understand how a graphing calculator can explore further when a function is continuous or discontinuous and know its limitations
  • identify the approximate roots of a function using the Intermediate Value Theorem

To meet these objectives you will review the following instructional videos:

  • The Definition and Properties of Continuous Functions
  • The Intermediate Value Theorem (IVT)
  • Continuity in Trigonometric Functions
