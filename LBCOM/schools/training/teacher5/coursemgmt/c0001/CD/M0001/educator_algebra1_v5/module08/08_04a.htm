08.04 Adding and Subtracting Rational Expressions

You will still be working with rational expressions throughout this lesson. So far, you have learned to simplify, multiply and divide them. Hopefully, you have also learned that rational expressions are fractions with variables in them. This means the operations on rational expressions follow the same rules as the operations on fractions.

In this lesson, you will learn to add and subtract rational expressions. So, in an effort to prepare your mind for these operations, take a look at some addition and subtraction of fractions.


As we mentioned in the previous lesson, at this point in your math career, you may have a good handle on addition and subtraction of fractions so the process below may seem tedious. However, be sure to read through it anyway because the "thought process" will lead you to a better understanding of how to add and subtract rational expressions.


Addition of fractions

When fractions have a common denominator, you should add the numerators and keep the same denominator. If fractions do NOT have a common denominator, you must make new fractions with the same denominator.
