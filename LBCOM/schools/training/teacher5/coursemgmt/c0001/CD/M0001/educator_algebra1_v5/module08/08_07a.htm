08.07 HONORS Part 2

Go the Extra Mile

You are almost there. Your destination is excellence.
You must go that extra mile.
You have learned to add and subtract rational expressions with different denominators. You will learn to solve more difficult equations using that knowledge.

We're off and running!!!


Example 1: Explanation:
LCD is x .
Multiply the fractions without the LCD by x/x to get common denominators .
Multiply each term in the equation by x (the denominator) to
clear the fractions .
Now you have a quadratic equation without fractions.
Get zero on the right by adding 5x to both sides of the equation.
Factor the polynomial on the left.
Set each factor equal to zero .
Solve both equations.
Check to be sure the solutions do not make the denominator equal zero in the original equation. These are good.
Finish checking the solutions.

Both solutions are correct.

Example 2: Explanation:
Factor to find the LCD .
The LCD is (y + 2)(y - 2).


Multiply the numerators and denominators of each term by whatever form of 1 will get the common denominators.
Now you have common denominators.
Multiply each term in the equation by that denominator to clear the equation of fractions.
Now you have a normal equation. Solve it. Be careful about the negative sign between terms. It must be distributed so use parentheses.
Distribute. Notice the negative?
Collect like terms.
Add -11 to both sides and -5y to both sides to get the variables on one side of the equation and the constants on the other side.
Divide both sides by -3 to solve.
Be certain that your answer does not make any denominators in the original equation equal to zero. This answer is good.
Complete the check. This answer is correct.
