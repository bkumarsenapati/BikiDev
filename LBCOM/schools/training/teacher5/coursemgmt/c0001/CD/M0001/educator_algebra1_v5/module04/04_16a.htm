04.16 Practice Problems

In Module 3, assignment 03.08 you practiced writing equations, but didn't have to solve them. You will now actually solve equations similar to the ones in 3.08. Before you continue, please go to your submission for 03.08 and print or write down your equations before you try to answer the questions below. Some of the equations from assignment 3.08 are identical to the ones below and some are very similar. This assignment will be graded by the computer and you will use your answers to complete this assignment.

  1. Joe is 5 years older than Sally. In 15 years Joe will be 3 times as old as Sally is now. How old is Sally now? Hint: Write Joe's age now in terms of Sally's age now. Then write Joe's age in 15 years using the expression you just wrote for Joe's age now. Then using S for Sally's age now, write the equation with only one variable "S" and solve it.

    1. 2
    2. 10
    3. 15
    4. 30
    5. 45

  2. The length of a rectangle is 3 more than twice its width. The perimeter is 48 feet. Find the width. Hint: You did this in Module 3 Assignment 5. Now you have to write the equation and solve it.
    1. 16
    2. 7
    3. 8
    4. 21
    5. 24

  3. Five times the first of three consecutive even integers is fourteen more than three times the second. What is the third consecutive integer? Hint: You did this in Module 3 Assignment 5. Now you have to write the equation and solve it.
    1. 10
    2. 12
    3. 14
    4. 16
    5. 18

  4. Fifteen more than four times a number is 6 more than five times the number. Find the number.
    1. 21/9
    2. -9
    3. -9/21
    4. 2
    5. 9

  5. Martha and Sally had lunch together on Tuesday. Sally paid $2 more than Martha. If the total bill was $5.64. How much did Sally pay for her lunch?
    1. $1.82
    2. $2
    3. $3.64
    4. $3.82
    5. $7.64

  6. Bob paid $3 less than Tim at a local pizza parlor. Together they spent $7.80. How much did Bob spend at the local pizza parlor?
    1. $2.40
    2. $4.50
    3. $4.80
    4. $5.40
    5. $8.40

  7. There were six more women on a committee than men. If the committee was made up of 30 people, how many men were on the committee?
    1. 6
    2. 12
    3. 24
    4. 30
    5. 36

  8. Three consecutive integers have the sum of 153. What is the second integer?
    1. 50
    2. 51
    3. 52
    4. 53
    5. 54

  9. The sum of three consecutive even integers is fifty-four. The sum of the first two consecutive integers is 34. What is the first consecutive integer?
    1. 13
    2. 14
    3. 15
    4. 16
    5. 17

  10. An airplane flies at a rate of 356 km/h . If the plane travels a total distance of 1424 km, how long was it in the air?
    1. 3.56 hr
    2. 4 hr
    3. 4.24 hr
    4. 5 hr
    5. 14.2 hr
