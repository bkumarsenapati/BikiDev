09.08 HONORS Problems

This is an Honors assignment. If you are not an Honors student, you do not need to complete this assignment.

Now is the time to put these inequalities to work for you! This assignment will require you to write some inequalities to represent real life situations. It is the writing of the inequalities that is of importance here.

For all the inequalities, you should choose a variable to represent the unknown value and then write an inequality that would allow you to find the value of the variable. You do not need to solve the inequalities.


The following situations can be written as single inequalities. You do not have to solve the inequalities.
  1. Eighteen is greater than or equal to four less than twice a number.
  1. Your quiz grades are 19, 17, 20, and 15. What is the lowest grade you can receive on the next quiz and maintain at least an 18 average?

    Help: Think about how to average grades. Use a variable to represent your score on the last quiz. Since you want your average to be at least an 18, you will use a > sign in your inequality. You want an average that is at least 18 but it could be more than 18.
  1. Stacey and Luis volunteer at the local hospital. Stacey worked 4 more hours than Luis, and together they worked more than 28 hours. What is the least number of hours each worked?

    Help: Use L to represent the number of hours worked by Luis. Write an expression in terms of L to represent the number of hours worked by Stacey. Write the inequality that represents the situation.
  1. Mrs. Culpepper has promised her two sons that they may go to a concert if together they save more than $45. The older son agrees to save twice as much as the younger one. How much must each save?

    Help: Use the same strategy as in number 3. Remember to write an inequality that has only one variable.

The following situations can be written as a system of inequalities. You should give a possible solution to the system of inequalities and can include a graph with your work.
  1. You must work for at least 80 hours in this course to complete the requirements. Some of that will be testing time, and some will be for assignments. You have 18 tests that take from 1/2 hour to 2 hours each to complete. You have 110 assignments that take between 1/2 hr and 1 hour to complete.
    1. Write the five inequalities involved.
    2. Try graphing the system of inequalities. This will be a challenge since the numbers are so big and there will be lots of overlaps. If you can submit a good handmade graph of this system, you will receive some extra credit points. If it seems to be taking too much of your time, simply move on to the next part of the problem.
    3. Choose a point that satisfies the constraints, and write a short paragraph about why you will devote these times to testing and assignments.

Help:

  1. One of the constraints is testing. Use x for the testing variable and y for the assignment variable. There are a total of 5 inequalities to write. 18 tests for 1/2 hour is 9 hours minimum. 18 tests for 2 hours each is 36 hours maximum. This gives you x > 9 and x < 36. Now write the inequalities that describe the time spent on assignments. The last inequality is the one for the
    total number of hours spent in the course (x + y).
  2. Once you have the inequalities, you will need to graph them on the same coordinate plane in order to find the area of intersection. If you are going to attempt to graph this system by hand, be sure to use appropriate tick marks on your coordinate plane. You may want to count by 5's so your graph will not be too big.
  3. The solutions to the system of inequalities are all the points in the overlapping shading areas. For example, you may work 20 hours on tests and quizzes and 100 hours on assignments. It is possible that due to your work on the assignments, you do not have to spend as much time on your quizzes and tests. Since 100 plus 20 is greater than 80 hours, you are meeting the total hour constraint as well.
  1. The school system is trying to help their students be more successful. They are going to hire tutors to help. They must have 20 teachers to have the correct student/teacher ratio. There must be no more tutors than teachers. The maximum number of people to be hired is 50. How many tutors and how many teachers should they hire?
    1. Write the inequalities for the above constraints.
    2. Try graphing the system of inequalities. These should be easier to graph by hand than number 5. If you can submit a good handmade graph of this system, you will receive some extra credit points. If it seems to be taking too much of your time, simply move on to the next part of the problem.
    3. Choose a point that satisfies the constraints, and write a short paragraph about why you chose that number of tutors and teachers.

    Help: Use x for teachers and y for tutors. There will be a total of three inequalities in this system.
