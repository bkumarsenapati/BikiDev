Module Five Lessons National Standards
05.00 Module Five Checklist and Pretest All Module Standards
05.01 Area Approximation and Riemann Sums Introduce Integrals with Archimedes’ Method of Exhaustion (numerical approximation) and how it leads to the natural use of the rectangle approximation method for the area under a curve; represent the area under a curve as a limit using sigma notation
05.02 Introduction to the Definite Integral Identify the definite integral as a limit of Riemann Sums; evaluate definite integrals by interpreting them geometrically; understand the differences or similarities, depending, between area (or “net signed area”) and the definite integral, and explore this concept using a graphing calculator; further exploring Riemann Sums and accumulated Change from a Rate of Change
05.03 The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus Use integrals to define functions, and explore that relationship; be able to take the derivatives of Integrals by the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus; use the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus to evaluate definite integrals
05.04 Integrals and Antiderivatives Evaluate indefinite integrals to find the general antiderivatives of functions
05.05 Integration by Substitution Evaluate indefinite integrals by use of the method of substitution; be able to evaluate definite integrals by use of the graphing calculator
05.06 The Definite Integral Evaluate definite integrals by use of the method of substitution; be able to evaluate definite integrals by use of the graphing calculator
