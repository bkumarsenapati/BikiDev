Circle Graph Example
The Next Mayor!

Five candidates entered a race for mayor and after a spirited campaign, the voters went to the polls.

The results (% of votes received for each candidate) from the primary election are presented in the circle graph to the right.

  • In the primary election, if a candidate receives a majority of the votes, then that person is elected mayor.
  • A majority of the votes means they must receive more than half of the votes.
  • If a candidate does not receive a majority of the votes in the primary election, then a run-off election is held.
  • The run-off election is between two candidates, the person receiving the most votes in the primary election and the person receiving the second most votes in the primary election.

Circle Graph Questions

  1. True/False: To receive the majority of the votes means to receive over 50% of the votes.
  2. True/False: Based on the information from the assignment, there not a need for a run-off election.
  3. Which candidate received the most votes in the primary election?
  4. Which candidate received the fewest votes in the primary election?
  5. If there is a run-off election, which candidates will be running?

Line Graph Example

Had enough of the summer heat? Let's head out west to snow country and Salt Lake City, Utah .

Decisions, decisions, decisions. Is this the year you try snowboarding or should you just keep skiing?

Arriving at Wasatch Ski and Board Rental, the owner of the shop Franz is also in the middle of a decision. Franz is unsure of how many sets of skis and how many snowboards he needs for the 1998 season. He knows that snowboarding is gaining in popularity and but he also knows that some ski resorts in the area, like Alta, do not allow snowboarding. (I guess that they think that the boarders are a little crazy!).

To help make a decision, Franz looks at the graph and table below that shows his ski and snowboard inventory for the past six seasons.

A ratio is a comparison of two or more quantities. Quantities are understood by assigning numeric values. So, a ratio is a comparison of two or more numbers. A ratio can be written in 3 different formats. If you were asked to write a ratio of skis to snowboards in 1995, you could write 225:5, 225 to 5 or 225/5. It is common to reduce ratios to lowest terms making the final answer 45:1, 45 to 1 or 45/1. You will include the number 1 because the ratio should compare at least two numbers.

Line Graph Questions

  1. What trend do you notice in ski inventory?
  2. What trend do you notice in snowboarding inventory?
  3. True/False: The ratio for 1995 of skis to snowboards is 25:1.
