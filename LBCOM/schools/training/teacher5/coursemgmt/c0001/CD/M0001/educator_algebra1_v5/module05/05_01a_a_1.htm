05.01 Linear Equations

The Coordinate Plane

The coordinate plane is a tool used for graphing algebraic relationships. The flat plane surface is divided by a horizontal line called the x axis and a vertical line called the y-axis. The x and y-axis intersect at the point (0,0) which is called the origin (the blue point in the illustration below). The x and y-axis divide the plane into four quadrants.

Quadrants
A point anywhere on the plane can be identified by a pair of numbers called the coordinate of the point. The coordinates are written inside parentheses and in a particular order. The x value is always given first and the y value second. This set of numbers written as (x,y) is called an ordered pair.

Ordered pairs

An ordered pair (x,y) is a pair of numbers with a particular order. Sounds easy enough, huh? It is important that you learn to graph an ordered pair and name an ordered pair from a graph.


Graphing ordered pairs

In an ordered pair, the x value is always listed first. It will represent the horizontal movement on the coordinate plane. A positive x-value means to move to the right and a negative x-value means to move to the left.

The y-value will always be listed second. It will represent the vertical movement on the coordinate plane. A positive y-value means to move up and a negative y-value means to move down.

Let's graph the following points.

(3,-2)

Go to the right 3
Go down 2

(0,4)

Do not move on the x-axis
Go up 4

(4,-5)

Go to the right 4
Go down 5

(-1,0)

Go to the left 1
Do not move on the y-axis


Naming Coordinates

Can you work backwards? You will need to know how to write the ordered pair for points on the coordinate plane. On the coordinate plane below, there are seven points shown. Write down the coordinates of each point on a piece of paper and then roll your cursor over the letters to see if you were right! You can do it.

Remember that x-coordinates are written first and represent the left or right value. The y-coordinates are written second and represent the up or down value.
