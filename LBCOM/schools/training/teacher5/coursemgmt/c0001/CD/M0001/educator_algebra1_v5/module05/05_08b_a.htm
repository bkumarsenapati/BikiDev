5.08 Honors Assignment Practice

Here are the four general steps to writing the equations for this assignment.

  1. Put the given equation in slope intercept form.
  2. Identify the slope and the given point of the new line. Parallel lines have the same slopes. Perpendicular lines have slopes that are negative reciprocals of each other.
  3. Write an equation of the new line using either the slope intercept or point slope form of a line.
  4. Put the equation in standard form.

1.
Step 1: x + 4y = 5
4y = -x + 5
y = -1/4x + 5/4

Step 2: The slope of the new line is -1/4.
The y-intercept is (0,-2)

Step 3: y = mx + b
y = -1/4x - 2

Step 4: 1/4x + y = -2
4 (1/4x + y = -2)
x + 4y = -8

2.
Step 1: 3x - 4y = 7
-4y = -3x + 7
y = 3/4x - 7/4

Step 2: The slope of the new line is 3/4.
The given point is (8, -1).

Step 3: y - y1 = m(x - x1)
y - (-1) = 3/4 (x - 8)
y + 1 = 3/4x - 6
y = 3/4x - 7

Step 4: -3/4x + y = -7
4 (-3/4x + y = -7)
-3x + 4y = -28
3x - 4y = 28

3.
Step 1: 4x - 5y = 1
-5y = -4x + 1
y = 4/5x - 1/5

Step 2: The slope of the new line is -5/4.
The given point is (-8,5).

Step 3: y - y1 = m(x - x1)
y - 5 = -5/4(x - (-8))
y - 5 = 5/4x - 10
y = -5/4x - 5

Step 4: 5/4x + y = -5
4 (5/4x + y = -5)
5x + 4y = -20

4.
Step 1: 2x + 7y = 3
7y = -2x + 3
y = -2/7x + 3/7

Step 2: The slope of the new line is 7/2.
The y-intercept is (0,6).

Step 3: y = mx + b
y = 7/2x + 6

Step 4: -7/2x + y = 6
2 (-7/2x + y = 6)
-7x + 2y = 12
7x - 2y = -12
