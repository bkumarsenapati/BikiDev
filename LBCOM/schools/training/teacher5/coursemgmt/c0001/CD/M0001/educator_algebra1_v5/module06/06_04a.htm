Multiplication of Polynomials
(Binomial x Binomial)

Multiplication can be a little more complex when you are multiplying a binomial (2 terms) and a binomial (2 terms). The idea is the same but now there is more to distribute. Let's use the problem (2x - 1)(x + 5) as an example.We must be sure to multiply every term in the first polynomial with every term in the second polynomial. Let's start with the 2x.

As shown in the illustration above, you must distribute. You will multiply the 2x times the x and the 2x times the 5 to get 2x2 + 10x.Now we will move on to the -1.

As shown in the illustration above, you must distribute. You will multiply the -1 times the x and the -1 times the 5 to get -1x - 5.To get the final answer, you must put it all together. There is an acronym to help you remember what to multiply. The acronym is FOIL. F stands for first, O stands for outer, I stands for inner and L stands for last. You will multiply the first two terms, the terms on the outside, the terms on the inside and the last two terms. See the illustration below.

2x2 + 10x - 1x - 5

Now collect like terms (the ones in the middle) to get your final answer.

2x2 + 9x - 5

Multiplication of Polynomials
(Polynomial X Polynomial)

Now you are ready for the big stuff. The last type of problem will involve the multiplication of many terms. Before we start, here are some important facts to remember.

  • x times x is x2. Since you are multiplying, you must ADD the exponents. x times x6 is x7. Since you are multiplying, you must ADD the exponents.2x plus 3x is 5x. Since you are adding, only the coefficient will change, NOT the exponents.
  • Terms must be alike to add them. 4x2 and 3x5 would not be like terms because the powers are not the same.

So, let's look at the last type of multiplication problem. The process is the same. You must multiply each term in the first parentheses with each term in the second parentheses. Here is an example. (x + 3)(2x2 - 5x + 7) We start by multiplying the x with all the terms in the second parentheses.

2x3 - 5x2 + 7x

The next step is to multiply the 3 with all the terms in the second parentheses.

6x2 - 15x + 21

Let's put it all together and collect the like terms. Remember that when you are collecting like terms, you are adding. Only the coefficients of the like terms will be added. The exponents will NOT change. The like terms are indicated by the similar color circles and squares.

2x3 - 5x2 + 7x + 6x2 - 15x + 21

2x3 + x2 - 8x + 21
