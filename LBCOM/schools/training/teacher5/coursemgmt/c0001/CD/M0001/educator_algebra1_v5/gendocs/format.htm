Lab reports
It is recommended that you print this page for future reference.

A lab report should be written in the following format:

Title (be as creative as you would like) (2 points)

Name (1 point)
E-mail address (1 point)

I. Materials: (5 points)
List of all the items you used to complete the lab.

II. Purpose: (5 points)
Several sentences explaining what you are trying to accomplish with this lab (This may be a connection or an example of Algebra in the world or just a practice exercise of skills learned in a module.)

Sample purpose for the Module 3 lab
(Please do not copy and paste this into your lab report but instead use it as a format for creating your own purpose)

"The purpose of this lab is to learn how to collect and analyze data. Learning how to use the MathResources graphing tool to make my first graph is an important part of the lab. I also used the order of operations to calculate the period of the pendulum from a formula. Finally, I learned the affects of both string length and weight of the bob on the period of the pendulum"

III. Procedure: (8 points)
List all the steps you took to perform the lab.

IV. Questions: (10 points)
Answer any questions in complete sentences, or in a table. You will find the questions in the assignment box at the bottom of the page.

V. Conclusion: (8 points)
Paragraph #1: Write a paragraph (3 sentences or more) of the outcome of the lab. An outcome includes the math concepts illustrated throughout the activity or a description of any patterns that occurred.
Paragraph #2: Write a paragraph (3 sentences or more) on your personal opinions of the lab. Was the lab difficult or easy? What did you learn? What could be changed to make the lab more clear for future students? Any other questions or concerns?

VI. Attachments: (10 points)
Attach any graphs, charts, or tables you have done for any part of this lab.
