08.01 Simplifying Rational Expressions

In this module, we will be working with rational expressions and equations. A rational expression has similar characteristics to a fraction. In fact, a rational expression is a fraction with variables in it.We learn how to perform operations on rational expressions in an effort to prepare for solving rational equations. Remember that all of Algebra is about solving equations and analyzing the graphs of the equations. So far, we have learned how to solve linear and quadratic equations. Working with rational expressions and equations will lead back to both of these algebraic concepts. Whether you move on to higher level high school math, plan on getting a 2- or 4-year degree at a college, or have a need in your job to work with Algebraic equations, you will find the ability to simplify, multiply, divide, add and subtract rational expressions a useful skill.

So, let's get started!


In this lesson, we will focus on simplifying rational expressions. Let's first think about how to simplify fractions. Remember that a rational expression is a fraction with variables in it. This means it will have the same characteristics as a fraction.

Below is an explanation of the "simplifying fractions" process. At this point in your math career, you may have a good handle on reducing fractions so the process below may seem tedious. HOWEVER, be sure to read through it anyway because the "thought process" will lead you to a better understanding of how to simplify rational expressions.

Let's simplify the fraction .The biggest key to your success here is to understand that simplifying involves factoring. Let's summarize.

  1. Factor the numerator and the denominator. Cancel the common factors.
  2. The final answer consists of the remaining factors.
