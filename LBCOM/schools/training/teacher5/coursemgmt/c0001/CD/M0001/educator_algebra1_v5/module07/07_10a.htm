07.10 HONORS Lab

This is an Honors Lab. If you are not an Honors student, you do not need to complete this lab.

Now is the time to put some formulas to work for you! This lab will illustrate the relationships between some great legends in the world of mathematics. The Pythagorean Theorem, Quadratic Formula and the Distance Formula all play a role in finding the lengths of sides and real life distances.


Directions for the Lab - Part 1

1. Collect the following materials:

  • Tape Measure
  • Calculator
  • Graph paper or computer grid

2. Measure the length and width of a cement block or some other rectangle shape in your home or neighborhood. Measure the diagonal of the block. Give all three measurements in inches.

The illustration shown below indicates the three measurements you will be taking.

3. Use the Pythagorean Theorem to calculate the length of the diagonal. Compare your calculated length with your measured length from the previous step. Are they similar?

4. Use your graph paper to construct a blueprint of your cement block. Place the left corner of your rectangle at the point (0,0) and label it point A.

Develop a scale. For example, one block on your grid represents 1 inch. Mark the other three corners of your rectangle as points B, C and D and label their coordinates.

6. Use your graph and your work to answer the questions below.

  1. What is the perimeter of the rectangle on your graph?
  2. What is the area of the rectangle on your graph?
  3. Tell how the Distance Formula and the Pythagorean Theorem are similar.

Directions for the Lab - Part 2

1. You have looked at the use of the Pythagorean Theorem and the Distance Formula in measurement. Now let's use the Quadratic Formula.

You have a room in your home 15 feet wide by 12 feet long and you want to increase its length and width by the same amount in order to have a total area of 350 square feet. Use the Quadratic Formula to find the dimensions of the new room.

Step 1: Let x represent the amount by which you will increase each side of your room. (see the illustration below)


Step 2: Write an equation for the area. Area equals length times width.

(x + 12)(x + 15) = 350

Step 3: Solve the equation. Your first step is to FOIL the left side of the equation. Secondly, subtract 350 from both sides so the equation is of the form ax2 + bx + c = 0. Finally, use the quadratic formula to find the value of x.

2. Use your work to answer the questions below.

  1. What is the value of x? Give your answer in decimal form. You can round it to the nearest hundredth.
  2. What is the length of the new room in decimal form?
  3. What is the width of the new room in decimal form?
  4. Use the Pythagorean Theorem to find the diagonal of the new room.
3. Print and write a lab report in the format you used for previous labs.
