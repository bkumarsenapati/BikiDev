Example 1 - Perimeter of a rectangle

(This example will help with number 2 in the assignment)
The length of a rectangle is one more than three times the width. The perimeter of the rectangle is equal to 58. Write an equation for the perimeter in terms of the width.

You do need some prior knowledge to write an equation for perimeter. The formula for perimeter is 2W + 2L = P. This means that to find the perimeter of a rectangle, you would add 2 widths and 2 lengths.

Let's take a look at how to set up the equation.

  • Your first step is to write your expressions for both the width and the length.
  • You cannot use L and W. You must use one letter.
  • In this case, we are going to use w as our variable.
  • To write an expression for length, you have to look at the words in the problem that explain the relationship between the length and the width.

Example 3 - Consecutive integers

(This example will help with numbers 7, 8 and 9 in the assignment)
Six times the first of three consecutive odd integers is equal to 3 less than 5 times the second.

Before we can write the equation, we need to learn how to write expressions for 3 consecutive odd integers. Review the illustrations below to learn how to write expressions for all types of consecutive integers.

Consecutive Integers

Consecutive integers are positive or negative whole numbers that come one right after the other. If the first number is represented by the letter x, then the second number must be x + 1 and the third number must be x + 2.
