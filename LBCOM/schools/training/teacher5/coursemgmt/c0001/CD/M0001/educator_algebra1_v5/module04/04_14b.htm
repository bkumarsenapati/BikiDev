04.14 Lab

You will Submit the following upon completion:

1) Algebra Lab Report completed
2.) Data table with new information added


Directions for the Racing Lab

Step 1: Collect the following materials:

    • Roller blades, skateboard, race car, or bicycle (if you don't have any of these items, you can always run)

    • Safety helmet

    • Tape

    • Stopwatch

    • Measuring tape

    • A friend

Write the prediction and a reason why you made the prediction you did.

Step 2: Measure a 25-foot distance on a smooth, level surface. Mark the beginning and the end of 25-foot distance with tape. A driveway, parking lot, sidewalk, or quiet street will work well. If you are using a race car instead of racing yourself, shorten the distance to 10 feet.

Step 3: Have a friend stand at the end of 25 feet with the stopwatch. Race from the start to the finish with roller blades, skateboard, or your chosen method of racing. Have your friend time you. Complete this racing step 10 times. You should have 10 times for a distance of 25 feet.

Don't forget your helmet and watch out for any traffic! We know how drivers can be!

TO DO: Have pencil and paper ready to record your times.

Step 4: You will need to use the information you collect in Step 3 to create a data table.

TO DO: Create a data table to organize all your data from the activity. Your data table should contain 11 rows and 3 columns. Label your columns: Distance, Time and Rate.

To determine the rate or speed at which you were traveling, use the formula Distance = Rate x Time (D = rt).Example

If your distance is 25 feet and your time is 30 seconds, then divide 25 by 30 to find your feet per second rate. Round your speed to the nearest hundredth.
25 divided by 30 = 0.83 feet per second

If you are unsure how to make and save a data table, return to lesson 2.01 Data Table for specific directions.

Step 5: You will now make a scatter plot from the data table in Step 4.

TO DO: Make a scatter plot graph from the data in your chart by clicking the link below. You do not have to save or submit this graph to your teacher. You'll need to use this graph to simply determine if your data points are linear (form a line when you plot them).

Scatter plot

Step 6: Write a lab report in the format you used for the Module 3 Lab.

TO DO: Print the format for your lab report.

Step 7: Answer the following questions based on your lab.

  1. Is the graph linear or non-linear?
  2. Is the relationship between time and rate of speed a direct or inverse relationship? Why? Think about what happens to the rate of speed as the time increases or decreases. You may want to look at the graphs and definitions in lesson 04.07 to help answer this question.
  3. What is the constant of variation? The constant of variation is the part of the experiment that always remained the same. It is the constant number in the equation D = rt.
  4. At the same distance of 25 feet, what would be the rate of speed of a person rollerblading for 60 seconds?

TO DO: Add the answers to your lab questions to the end of your lab report.

CONGRATULATIONS!!!! You did it!
