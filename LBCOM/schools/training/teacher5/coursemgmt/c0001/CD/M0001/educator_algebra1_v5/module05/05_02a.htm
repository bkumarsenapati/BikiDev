05.02 Slope and Intercepts

You have learned about linear equations in general. You have seen that linear equations come in many forms but all result in a straight line when the graph of the solution sets is constructed.

Certain forms of the linear equations are easier to work with than others.

The first form we will investigate is the Slope Intercept Form of a linear equation. Examples of this form are:

y = 3x + 4

y = -x +2

y = x - 5

The general Slope Intercept Form is: y = mx +b

Note- the m is the number before the x and the b is the number after the x. Let's see if we can discover what the m and b stand for in the equation.

Knowing the slope of a line can help us make important decisions.

Making decisions using your knowledge of slope.

1. You and your friend want to build a ramp for a wheelchair because you are helping an elderly neighbor. She is not very strong, so should you build a ramp with a slope of 3 or a slope of 6?

2. While planning a ski trip, you decide to ski the steepest mountain possible. The resort posts the slopes of Mountain A as , Mountain B as 2, and Mountain C as 5. Which mountain will grant your broken leg wish fastest..well..which is the steepest?


You can look at a linear equation that is in slope intercept form and tell the steepness of the line. This helps in decision making.

So, given the lines y = 3x -4 and y = x - 4, which line has the steepest slope and why?

Yes, the line y = 3x -4 has the steepest slope because the m value, or slope, is the greatest.

More to know about the y intercept

The b value in the linear equation in slope intercept form (y = mx + b) tells us where the line crosses the y.

In the line y = 3x -2, the line crosses the y axis at -2.

BUT>>>> points are named with an x and a y value. -2 is the y value, what is the x value when any line crosses the y axis?

Look at the graph of y = 3x - 2 using the slope tool shown above. Locate the point where the line crosses the y axis. What are the coordinates of the point?

Yes, the x value is 0 so the point where the line crosses the y axis is (0, -2)


Speaking of Intercepts

You now know that the y intercept is the place where the line crosses the y axis. If you look anywhere on the y axis, the value of x is 0.

Let's take this intercept knowledge one more step. What are the coordinates of a point where a line crosses the x axis. What do we know about the x intercept?

We know that the line can cross the x intercept on any point on the x axis. The x value will be the location on the x axis where the line crosses. What is the value of y anywhere on the x axis? Yes, the y coordinate is 0 when you are on the x axis.
