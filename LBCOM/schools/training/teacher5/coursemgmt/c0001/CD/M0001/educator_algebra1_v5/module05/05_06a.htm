05.06 Writing Equations of Lines

To write an equation of a line, you must have the slope of the line and a point that lies on the line.

Please read this entire lesson very carefully. What you are about to learn is an important skill you will need to know.

There will be three types of problems illustrated in this lesson. You will learn:

  • How to write an equation of a line when given the slope and the y-intercept. (You already know all you need for this.)
  • How to write an equation of a line when given the slope and a point on the line.
  • How to write an equation of a line when given two points on the line.

Writing Equations when you know the slope and the y intercept

EASY! Use the slope intercept form of an equation. y = mx + b where m is the slope and b is the y-intercept.


Example #1: Write the equation of a line with a slope of 2 and a y-intercept of (0,-3).

y = mx + b
y = 2x - 3

That's it! Can it be that easy? Yes it can. All you do is substitute the slope in the place of m and the y-intercept in the place of b.

Let's do one more.

Example #2: Write the equation of a line with a slope of - and a y intercept of (0,4).

y = mx + b

y = - x + 4

Writing equations of a line when given the slope and a point on the line

You need the slope (m) and the y intercept (b) to write the equation of a line in y = mx + b form. Always keep this in mind. Let's do some examples.


Example #1: Write the equation of a line with a slope of 2 and passing through the point (-3,4).

Remember, y = mx + b.

We know m is 2 and (-3, 4) is a point on the line. (-3, 4) is an ordered pair of the form x,y. So, we will use the equation y = mx + b and plug in 2 for m, -3 for x and 4 for y, in order to solve for b.

Plug and chug:
y = mx + b
4 = 2(-3) + b
4 = -6 + b [add 6 to both sides to solve for b]
10 = b

Substitute m and b into the Slope Intercept form: y = 2x + 10

Example #2: Write the equation of a line with a slope of -3 and passing through point (4, 7).

Plug and chug:
y = mx + b
7 = -3(4) + b
7 = -12 + b [add 12 to each side to solve for b]
19 = b

Substitute m and b into the Slope Intercept form: y = -3x + 19   


There is another way to write the equation of a line given a point and the slope. It uses a formula called the point-slope formula. The point-slope formula is y - y1 = m(x - x1).

In order to use this formula you will be given the slope "m" and a point on the line (x1, y1).

Writing equations of a line when given two points on the line

The hardest but not hard: It always comes back to the Slope Intercept form of a line y = mx + b.

Think - you need the slope (m) and the y intercept (b) and then you can plug those values into the equation and you are done.

We do not have the slope but we know how to find it if we have two points.

We do not have the y intercept but we can find it if we know the slope and a point on the line. (just did it in the last part)

Step 1: find the slope (m)
Step 2: find the y intercept (b) using the slope (m) and a point on the line
Step 3: plug the m and the b into y = mx +b and you are done
