Lesson Objectives

After completing this lesson, you will be able to:

  • understand the format of free response questions on the exam
  • review a list of topics frequently tested in the FRQ section

Exam Description

The Format of the Free Response (FRQ) Section

Section II of the AP Calculus BC Exam consists of the free response questions split into two parts: calculator Part A and non-calculator Part B. Not all questions in Part A will actually require the use of a calculator. In Part A you will be asked to solve two problems in 30 minutes; in Part B you will solve four questions in 60 minutes, so you should practice completing a free response question in 15 minutes. Once Part A is over, you will be asked to put away your calculator, and you will then take Part B. If you finish the four non-calculator questions in Part B early, you may go back and continue to work on Part A, but you will not be permitted to use your calculator. Each question is worth nine points and has multiple parts, but the parts are not weighted equally. You may use pencil or pen, and you must write in the space provided. Don't worry! The answer booklet gives you plenty of room to show your work.
