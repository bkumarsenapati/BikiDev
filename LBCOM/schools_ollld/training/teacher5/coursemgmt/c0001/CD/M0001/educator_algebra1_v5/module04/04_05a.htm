04.05 Solving Equations with Fractions

When you find an equation that contains a fraction, sometimes it is best to eliminate that fraction in order to help you solve the equation easier.

Let's go through each of these types one at a time.

Type 1: The equation contains a fraction in it. (You have already done fraction problems like this one in earlier lessons. We are going to learn to do them another way here.)

To eliminate fractions, multiply EVERY term on both sides of the equation by the least common denominator for the fraction(s) in the equation.

LCD = 4

 

In this problem, there is only one fraction and its denominator is 4. So, each term in the equation is multiplied by the 4. You can think of the as in order to do the multiplication.

3x - 8 = -56

Here is what you have left after multiplying every term with a 4. This is now a simple 2 step equation.


       3x - 8 = -56
3x - 8 + 8 = -56 + 8
            3x = -48
         
               x = -16

Read the left side of the equation aloud to yourself. It says, "three times x minus 8". So, we must undo the "times 3" and the "minus 8". We will add the number 8 and then divide by the number 3.


Type 2: One side of the equation is a big fraction.

To eliminate fractions, multiply EVERY term on both sides of the equation by the least common denominator for the fraction(s) in the equation.

LCD = 5

 

 

The LCD is the number 5.

 

In this case, the fraction is considered one "term" so it will act as a single term when being multiplied by the common denominator, 5. You can think of the as in order to do the multiplication. The 5's cancel and you have (x-2) left.

x - 2 = 35

Here is what you have left after multiplying every term with a 5. This is now a simple 1 step equation.

x - 2 + 2 = 35 + 2
            x = 37

Add the number 2 to both sides and solve for x.


Type 3: There is a fraction multiplied with a parentheses set.

There are several ways to start this problem. Probably, the most obvious way for students would be to multiply every term in the parentheses with the ½.


        

 

 

Now, simplify each part on the left side.

 

The part can be thought of as . This simplifies to the number 2. This is now a type 1 fraction problem.

          

       x - 4 = 40
x - 4 + 4 = 40 + 4
            x = 44

The LCD for the fraction in this problem is the number 2. So, we will multiply each term on both sides by the number 2.

 

This gives us the equation x - 4 = 40. Add 4 to both sides and solve for x.

LCD = 10

We are going to multiply every term on both sides by the number 10.


            15x = 2x -78

Notice how you can write the 10 as a when you multiply it with each term.

Now, you have an equation with variables on both sides.



       15x = 2x - 78
15x - 2x = 2x - 2x - 78
       13x = -78
    
            x = -6

Subtract the 2x from each side to get your variable terms together. Finally, divide by the 13 and you have solved for x.


Travel Advisory:

 

Solving Equation Strategy

 

  • Apply the Distributive Property if there are any numbers or variables waiting to be multiplied with terms in parentheses.
  • Eliminate any fractions by multiplying EVERY TERM on both sides by the LCD for the fraction(s).
  • Combine like terms on each side of the equation if there are any.
  • Move any variable terms to the left side of the equation and all constant terms to the right side of the equation. (Constants are terms containing only numbers, no variables.)
  • Solve for x.
