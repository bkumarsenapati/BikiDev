01.04 Your Travel To Do List

Completion of Travelogues (Assignments)

Please refer to your schedule for the number of weekly assignments that you must submit.

You will be allowed to do an assignment two times. The first submission will be reviewed by your instructor. If it is correct, your instructor will assign a final grade. If some of the work is not correct, your instructor will make comments. You should make the corrections and then submit the work for a final grade.

You will be allowed to take tests and quizzes one time. Be sure to review prior to taking an assessment. Once you enter the test or quiz, your instructor will receive a submission from you. You are not allowed to enter the test and then cancel out of it.

Daily Travel Tasks

There are some very important tasks that you should complete every time you begin to work:

  1. Check your email daily. Your instructor will send a lot of valuable information through group emails.

  2. Check for reviewed work. If you have any zeros or low grades on your work, you will need to make the corrections and resubmit the work for a better grade.

  3. Check your work for comments from your instructor. Even though an assignment has a final grade, it may have some important comments.

  4. Enter your grades onto your Itinerary chart. Check your grades from the gradebook. Contact your instructor if there are discrepancies.

  5. Study before you take a test or quiz. You will get one submission of each test or quiz so you want to be prepared.

Questions for Your Travel Agent

You must be willing to call your teacher for help. Your instructor will not know you are struggling with a lesson unless you contact him/her. The phone is the best way to get help with an assignment.

There are some things you can do before you call.

Read the lesson. Read every word and example. Do not skim the lesson with the thought, "I will never get this."

Try the practice problems (when applicable) and check the solutions to see how much you know.

Try to call with specific questions about the lesson.
