Solving Pure Quadratic Equations

The example at the right explains the procedure on solving a "pure" quadratic equation. These problems are simplified because they do not contain the bx term (known as the linear term). It only contains the squared term!

First solve the equation for x2. Then the number on the right is square rooted. The one thing you must remember is that there are 2 solutions (or roots) to a quadratic equation. When we worked on square roots earlier in this module, we were interested in only the principle square root or positive square root. Now, we need to identify both solutions: the positive and negative!
