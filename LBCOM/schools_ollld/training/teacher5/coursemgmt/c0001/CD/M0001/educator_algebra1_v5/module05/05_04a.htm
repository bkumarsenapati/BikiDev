05.04 Special Lines

Wow! You have come a long way on the slippery slope of knowledge! Let's learn about lines that you have seen but have not thought about in terms of slope.

Horizontal and Vertical lines have unique equations and slopes. In this lesson you will learn to:

  • Recognize horizontal and vertical lines from both their graphs and their equations.
  • Determine the slopes of horizontal, vertical lines, parallel and perpendicular lines.
  • Write equations for horizontal, vertical lines parallel and perpendicular lines.
Equations of Horizontal Lines

Horizontal Lines are written a bit differently then the ones you saw in the previous lesson. Notice there is no variable "x" in the equation of a horizontal line. These lines are always expressed as "y equals a number".

y = 3    or      y = -4


Let's look at the linear equation: y = 3

Table of values:

For every x value selected, y is always going to equal 3 because the equation says "y equals 3 ".

Note: Look at several points on the line. The x values change and no matter what value of x is chosen, y is always 3. The x values go in the horizontal direction so you get a horizontal line showing all the possible x values.

Graph:

Notice that graph pictured above crosses the y-axis at 3.

Solutions:

Any ordered pair that is on the graph of the line y = 3 has a y-value of 3. These points are considered solutions of the equation y = 3.


Equations of Vertical Lines

Vertical Lines are written a bit differently then the ones you saw in the previous lesson. Notice there is no variable "y" in the equation of a vertical line. These lines are always expressed as "x equals a number".

x = -2   or     x = 1


Let's look at the linear equation: x = -2

Table of values:

The only value of x that can be selected is x = -2. In this equation, it does not matter what y is because the equation says "x equals negative 2".

Note: Look at several points on the line. The y values change and no matter what value of y is chosen, x is always -2. The y values go in the vertical direction so you get a vertical line showing all the possible y values.

Graph:

Notice this graph crosses the x-axis at negative 2.

Solutions:

Any ordered pair that is on the graph of the line x = -2 has an x-value of -2. These points are considered solutions of the equation x = -2.
