07.06 Trinomials Part 2

You are probably expecting to see our factoring flow chart again, right? Since this lesson is a continuation of factoring Trinomials, we are not going to show the chart again. Lets simply continue our exploration of Trinomials.

In the last lesson, you learned how to factor trinomials of the form x2 + bx + c. In this lesson, you are going to learn how to factor trinomials that contain a "leading coefficient". A leading coefficient means the x2 term will now have a coefficient other than one in front of it.


Factoring Trinomials of the form ax2 + bx + c.

Here are some important points about these trinomials.

  • These trinomials start off with a coefficient in front of the first term.
  • Notice how the powers of x descend. That means they work down from x2 to the last term having no x at all.
  • Sometimes these trinomials start with powers of x higher than x2... like x4, or x6.
  • Sometimes these trinomials contain two variables. Usually, the first variable will be descending (going from high powers down to lower powers) and the second variable will be ascending (going from a lower power up to a higher power).

How do you factor trinomials of the form ax2 + bx + c?

There are 2 ways to factor this type of trinomial:

  1. You can factor these trinomials by guessing and checking like you did in the last lesson. However, you must consider the way the leading coefficient affects the outcome of the factoring process. The correct factorization will not be as obvious as it was before.
  2. You can follow a more systematic approach to factoring these trinomials called factoring by grouping. This method is similar to the process we learned in project 7.03 for factoring polynomials with four terms.

Factoring ax2 + bx + c by guessing and checking.
When you factor by guessing and checking you can use the same sign rules you learned in lesson 7.04.

Sign rules for Factoring Trinomials:

  • If the last term in the trinomial is positive, the signs in the factored answer will be the same.
    • If the middle term is positive, the signs in the factored answer will both be positive.
    • If the middle term is negative, the signs in the factored answer will both be negative.
  • If the last term in the trinomial is negative, the signs in the factored answer will be different.

 Example 1:

8x2 + x - 7

=(    +    )(     -     )

Step 1: Determine the signs of the factored answer.

Since the last term is negative, we know the signs in our parentheses will be different.

(x +   )(8x -   )
(8x +   )(x -   )
(4x +   )(2x -   )
(2x +   )(4x -   )

Here are your four possibilities based on the signs in the problem and the possible factors for 8x2.

Step 2: Determine the different ways you can break the first term into two factors.

8x2 can be written as:

x times 8x      or      2x times 4x

 
(x + 7)(8x - 1)

(x + 1)(8x - 7)

(8x + 7)(x -1)
(8x + 1)(x -7)
(4x + 7)(2x - 1)
(4x + 1)(2x - 7)
(2x + 7)(4x - 1)

(2x + 1)(4x - 7)

Here our the possible factors for this trinomial.

Step 3: Determine the different ways you can break the last term into two factors.

7 can only be written as 7 times 1.

(x + 7)(8x - 1) = 8x2 + 55x - 7

(x + 1)(8x - 7) = 8x2 + x - 7

(8x + 7)(x -1) = 8x2 - x - 7
(8x + 1)(x -7) = 8x2 - 55x - 7
(4x + 7)(2x - 1) = 8x2 - 10x - 7
(4x + 1)(2x - 7) = 8x2 -26x - 7
(2x + 7)(4x - 1) = 8x2 + 26x - 7

(2x + 1)(4x - 7) = 8x2 -10x - 7

The correct factored answer is
(x + 1)(8x - 7) = 8x2 + x - 7.

Step 4: Work through the possible combinations until you find the pair of factors that can FOIL back to the original trinomial.

Now, you do not have to list all of these possibilities. You can simply, write down the parentheses, put in the correct signs and then start manipulating the terms.

  • The first terms in each parentheses will multiply to give you the first term in the trinomial.
  • The last terms in the parentheses will multiply to give you the last term in the answer.
  • Check your guess by FOILing. Make sure you have the right combination of terms to create the correct middle term in the trinomial.
Factoring ax2 + bx + c by grouping.

 Example 2:

2x2 -7x + 6

2(6) = 12

Step 1: Multiply the first and the last coefficients together.

Step 2: List all the factor pairs for the number +12.

Step 3: Find the pair of numbers that will multiply to give you +12 but add up to the middle coefficient, -7.

  2x2 - 7x + 6

= 2x2 - 3x - 4x + 6

Step 4: Use the pair of numbers you found in step 3 to write the original trinomial as a four-term polynomial. Do this by taking the middle term, -7x and writing it as two terms, -3x -4x.

    2x2 - 3x      - 4x + 6

= x(2x - 3)      -2(2x - 3)

= (2x - 3) (x - 2)

Step 5: Now that you have a polynomial with 4 terms, you can follow the grouping process from project 7.03.

Break the problem into two parts and factor out the GCF for each part. Then, if the parentheses match, factor out the parentheses as a GCF for the entire problem.

(2x - 3)(x - 2) = 2x2 - 4x - 3x + 6

                       =  2x2 - 7x + 6

Step 6: Check your guess by using the FOIL process to verify that the factored answer does multiply back to 2x2 - 7x + 6. 

 Example 3:

5x2 + 8xy - 4y2

5(-4) = -20

Step 1: Multiply the first and the last coefficients together.

Step 2: List all the factor pairs for the number -20.

Step 3: Find the pair of numbers that will multiply to give you -20 but add up to the middle coefficient, +8.

   5x2 + 8xy - 4y2

= 5x2 +10xy - 2xy - 4y2

Step 4: Use the pair of numbers you found in step 3 to write the original trinomial as a four-term polynomial. Do this by taking the middle term, 8xy and writing it as two terms, 10xy -2xy.

   5x2 +10xy    - 2xy - 4y2

= 5x(x + 2y)    -2y(x + 2y)

= (x + 2y)(5x - 2y)

Step 5: Now that you have a polynomial with 4 terms, you can follow the grouping process from project 7.03.

Break the problem into two parts and factor out the GCF for each part. Then, if the parentheses match, factor out the parentheses as a GCF for the entire problem.

(x + 2y)(5x - 2y) = 5x2 -2xy + 10xy - 4y2

                        =  5x2 + 8xy - 4y2

 Step 6: Check your guess by using the FOIL process to verify that the factored answer does multiply back to 5x2 + 8xy - 4y2.

 Example 4:

8x2 - 14x + 5

8(5) = 40

Step 1: Multiply the first and the last coefficients together.

Step 2: List all the factor pairs for the number +40.
Step 3: Find the pair of numbers that will multiply to give you +40 but add up to the middle coefficient, -14.

  8x2 - 14x + 5

= 8x2 - 4x -10x + 5

Step 4: Use the pair of numbers you found in step 3 to write the original trinomial as a four-term polynomial. Do this by taking the middle term, -14x and writing it as two terms, -4x - 10x.

   8x2 - 4x       -10x + 5

= 4x(2x - 1)     -5(2x - 1)

= (2x - 1) (4x - 5)

Step 5: Now that you have a polynomial with 4 terms, you can follow the grouping process from project 7.03.

Break the problem into two parts and factor out the GCF for each part. Then, if the parentheses match, factor out the parentheses as a GCF for the entire problem.

(2x - 1)(4x - 5)= 8x2 - 10x - 4x + 5

                       =  8x2 - 14x + 5

Step 6: Check your guess by using the FOIL process to verify that the factored answer does multiply back to 8x2 - 14x + 5.
