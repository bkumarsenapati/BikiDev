Properties The algebraic properties are used in solving equations and in proofs. They are the rules by which we can distribute, collect like terms, add to each side of an equation and multiply or divide to find the value of x. We must always follow the rules to find the correct answer.
Distribution Distribution is the first key to correctly solving an equation. If you miss one sign in distribution, you will get the wrong answer. You must pay close attention to all the details from beginning to end. Concentration is the key.
Equations Solving equations: This web site shows you how to solve equations. Please work problems under solving equations 1, 2, & 3. For honor students, 4 & 5 are appropriate. Work at least 2 problems for each example. That's 6 problems for Algebra, 10 problems for honor students.
Functions Functions and relations are both very important in Algebra. We deal mostly with functions in Algebra 1, but in Algebra 2 you deal with many relations. Equations of the form y = mx + b or y = ax2 + bx + c are functions. The first is a linear function, the second is a quadratic function. There are seven practice problems here.
Graphing There are three ways to graph linear equations without a software tool. Click on the icon at the left for a review of those methods and the associated concepts. There are six problems here.
Slopes A slope is part of an equation. There are two forms of an equation involving slope: the slope-intercept form: y = mx + b, and the point- slope form: y - y1= m(x - x1). Click on the icon at the left for a thorough review of slopes.
Parallel Lines and Perpendicular Lines

This is an honors section of the review.

Parallel and perpendicular lines have special slope relationships. You can write the equation of a line with only information about one point, if you know that line is parallel or perpendicular to another line, and you have the equation of that other line.

Systems of Equations To solve for two variables you need two equations. This is a review of solving systems of equations. There are some practice problems.
