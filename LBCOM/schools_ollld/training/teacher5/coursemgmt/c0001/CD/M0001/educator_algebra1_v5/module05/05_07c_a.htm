Graphing Equations in Slope Intercept Form - Answers

Line 1

m = 1/2

b = +2

  • First graph the y-intercept, 2.
  • Next, the slope is 1/2 so you count up1 unit (rise) and to the right 2 units (run).
  • Finally, connect the points to make a line.

        


Line 2

m = -3/2 

b = -2

  • First graph the y-intercept, -2.
  • Next, the slope is -3/2 so count down 3 units and to the right 2 units . Note: the change in y is -3 indicating that you count down 3 units.
  • Finally, connect the points to make a line.

         

Line 3

y = -2x + 2

m = -2  

b = 2

  • First graph the y-intercept, 2.
  • Next, the slope is -2, so count down 2 units and to the right 1 unit. Note: the change in y is -2 indicating you count down 2 units. Since the slope is not a fraction, we think of the number -2 as -2/1. The 1 tells us to move 1 unit to the right.
  • Finally, connect the points to make a line.

           y = -2x + 2

Converting Equations from Standard Form to Slope Intercept Form - Answers

Line 7

15x + 5y = 10

First convert to slope intercept form.

Isolate the y term (5y)
15x - 15x + 5y = -15x + 10
Result is: 5y = -15x + 10
Divide by 5
Result is: y = -3x + 2

Now, graph the line using the slope and the y-intercept.

15x + 5y = 10  converted to
             y = -3x + 2
