Making your first graph
It is recommended that you print this page for future reference.

1. Enter your data from the lab activity into the T-chart.

A T-chart is the x|y chart that you see below the graph window. Use the length of the string for your x value and the period of the pendulum for your y value. Do not enter any words in the T-chart.

Even though you can only see 5 rows in the view, you will be able to enter all 10 points by simply pressing enter after the 5th entry. In the example, only 5 points were entered.

2. Click on the pencil icon at the top of the screen to create the graph.

3. It is important to create a professional and accurate graph. Below are some tips for making your graph look its best.

The graph may appear "squished." To fix this problem, choose unequal scaling from the toolbar.

Be sure to LABEL your graph. Choose Edit, Options, Labels, Axes... from the toolbar.

Enter the title of your graph and the labels for the x and y axis.

4. You must save your graph and get a copy of it to your instructor. There are several options to accomplish this task. Please choose the one that is appropriate for you.

Option 1
If you made your data table for the lab in a word processing program, you can copy and paste your graph from the MathResources graphing tool into the same document as your data table. Choose File, Image, Copy from the toolbar. Then go to your data table file and press CTRL V to paste the graph.

Option 2
If you made your data table for the lab in a spreadsheet, then you will have to save the graph as a separate file and send it as an attachment to email. You will NOT be able to attach 2 files in the assignment area. Please attach your data table to the assignment area and send your graph from the graphing tool through email or by fax.

It is important that you save your file in a format that your instructor can open.
Choose File, Image, Export.

Save your graph as a bitmap file.
Remember to save it in your Algebra folder that you created in Assignment 01.03. If you have not made a folder and need instructions for doing so, click here.
