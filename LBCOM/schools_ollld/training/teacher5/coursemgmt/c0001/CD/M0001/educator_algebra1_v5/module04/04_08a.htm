04.08 Literal

Any equation that consists of many variables (for example d, r, and t in the equation d = rt) is called a literal equation. You have heard that word "literal" before in literacy, literature, and literary.

What does "literal" mean?

A Literal Equation is an equation with letters rather than numbers. The most important thing to keep in mind is that you will be using the same steps to solve literal equations that you use to solve equations with numbers. Try not to let the letters fool you.

Remember to do the opposite math operations and to keep your equation balanced.
