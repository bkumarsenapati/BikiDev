05.08 Honors Practice Answers Part 1

Number 1

y = 5x + 11  
y - 5x = 5x - 5x + 11 (Subtract 5x from both sides)
-5x + y = 11 (Use commutative property on left side of the equation and simplify the right side)
-1(-5x + y) = -1(11) (Multiply both sides of the equation by -1 to make "a" positive)
5x - y = -11  
 

Number 2

y = - x - 4 (add x to both sides)
y + x = -x + x - 4 (Use commutative property on the left side of the equation and simplify the right side)
x + 7 = -4 (Multiply both sides of the equation by 2 to clear the fraction)
2(x + y) = 2(-4)  
x + 2y = -8  

Number 3

y = x + 1  
y - x = x - x + 1 (Subtract x from both sides)
-x + y = 1 (Use commutative property on the left side of the equation and simplify the right side)
-7 (-x + y) = -7(1) (Multiply both sides of the equation by negative 7 to clear fractions and make "a" positive)
2x - 7y = -7
