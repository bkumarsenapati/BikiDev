07.07 Factoring Completely Practice

Practice Problems

Here are some practice examples for you to try to factor on your own paper.

  • Remember to look for a GCF before trying to factor.
  • Don't forget to check your factored answer to make sure it is completely factored.
  • Write "prime" if the polynomial is not factorable. (This means you were not able to factor the problem at all. If you were able to take out a GCF, you cannot say "prime". You were able to factor something.)
