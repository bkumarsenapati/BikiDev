05.11 Solving Systems of Equations

a)

4x - 3y = -10
2x + 3y = 4

Add these two linear equations together. Notice when you do this, the 3y's will cancel out:
4x - 3y = -10
2x + 3y = 4
________
6x = -6
/6 /6
x = -1

Now, we can take x = -1 and plug it back into either one of the original equations. I'll choose the first one:
4x - 3y = -10
4(-1) - 3y = -10
-4 - 3y = -10
+4 +4
-3y = -6
/-3 /-3
y = 2

So our final answer is (-1,2). Remember to write your final answer as an ordered pair. Remember that graphically you are finding the point where the two lines intersect.

b)

2x + 3y = 2
9x - 3y = 42

Add these two linear equations together. Notice when you do this, the 3y's will cancel out:

2x + 3y = 2
9x - 3y = 42
_________
11x = 44
/11 /11
x = 4

Now, we can take the x = 4 and plug it back into either one of the original equations. I'll choose the first one:

2x + 3y = 2
2(4) + 3y = 2
8 + 3y = 2
-8 -8
3y = -6
/3 /3
y = -2

So the answer is (4, -2).
