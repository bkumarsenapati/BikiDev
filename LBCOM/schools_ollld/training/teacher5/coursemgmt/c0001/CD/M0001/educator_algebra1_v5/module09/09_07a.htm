09.07 Systems

Take a look at inequalities on a coordinate plane!

Remember y = mx + b! The equation of a line can be transformed into an inequality. In Module 5, you learned how to graph equations of a line. The graphing of inequalities is very similar. In this lesson, you will learn how to graph both single inequalities and systems of inequalities

Systems of inequalities are often used in the business world. Profit and cost regions are an everyday experience for a business. A profit and cost region is the area where you can make a profit while still keeping costs at a minimum. They call this Linear Programming! But before we do any fancy analysis, let's practice our graphing.


Graphing a linear inequality


Example 1

Let's graph -2x + y < 1.

The first step is to ensure that the inequality is in slope intercept form. To put this inequality into the slope intercept form, you must add 2x to both sides.

The slope intercept form of the inequality is y < 2x + 1.

Now, we can identify the slope and the y-intercept allowing us to graph the inequality.
m = 2 and the y-intercept is (0,1)

Graphing a system of linear inequalities

Systems of inequalities are two inequalities that will intersect at a shaded region containing the solutions. To graph and solve a system of inequalities, you will graph each inequality and then find the place where the two shaded areas overlap.

Recall the battleship lab in Module 5. You were asked to find the point of intersection of two lines to determine where the mines would be placed. This is the same concept but you will have a shaded intersection rather than a single point.


Example 1

Solve and graph the system of inequalities.
-x + y < 1 and x + y > -3

The inequalities should be graphed on the same coordinate plane. First, let's put them in slope intercept form and identify the slope and y-intercept for the purpose of graphing.

-x + y < 1
Slope intercept form is y < x + 1

The slope is 1 and the y-intercept is (0,1).

x + y > -3
Slope intercept form is y > -x - 3

The slope is -1 and the y-intercept is (0,-3).


Now we will graph them on the same coordinate plane.
y < x + 1 is graphed in blue with yellow shading. It is a dotted line with shading below the line.
y > -x - 3 is graphed in red with green shading. It is a solid line with shading above the line.

The solutions to the system of inequalities are found in the yellow AND green shaded area. This is the place of intersection for the inequalities.

The point (8,2) would be a solution to the system and would work in both inequalities.

There are an infinite number of points in the shaded area so there are an infinite number of solutions to the system.


Example 2

Solve and graph the system of inequalities.
-2x + 5y > 10 and 2x + y > 4

The inequalities should be graphed on the same coordinate plane. First, let's put them in slope intercept form and identify the slope and y-intercept for the purpose of graphing.

-2x + 5y > 10
Slope intercept form is y > (2/5)x + 2

The slope is 2/5 and the y-intercept is (0,2).

2x + y > 4
Slope intercept form is y > -2x + 4

The slope is -2 and the y-intercept is (0,4).


Now we will graph them on the same coordinate plane.

  • y > (2/5)x + 2 is graphed in blue with yellow shading. It is a solid line with shading above the line.
  • y > -2x + 4 is graphed in red with green shading. It is a solid line with shading above the line.

The solutions to the system of inequalities are found in the yellow AND green shaded area. This is the place of intersection for the inequalities.

The point (-2, 10) would be a solution to the system and would work in both inequalities.

There are an infinite number of points in the shaded area so there are an infinite number of solutions to the system.
