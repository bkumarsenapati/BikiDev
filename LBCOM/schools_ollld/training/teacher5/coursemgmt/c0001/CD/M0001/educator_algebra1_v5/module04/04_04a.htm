04.04 Advanced Equations

Here are some general steps for solving more advanced equations.

Step 1: Distribute on each side of the equation.
Step 2: Collect like terms on each side of the equation.
Step 3: Get all the variables on the same side of the equation.
Step 4: Solve for the variable.

Special Cases
Special Case: Any number
Special case: No Solution


2x - 6 = 2(x - 3)
2x - 6 = 2x - 6

If you subtract 2x from both sides of the equation, you will get
-6 = -6

Since the statement -6 = -6 is always true, any real number can be substituted for x to make the original equation a true statement. For this case, the solution is "any real number."


2x - 6 = 2(x - 4)
2x - 6 = 2x - 8

If you subtract 2x from both sides of the equation, you will get
-6 = -8

Since -6 = -8 is always false, there is no number that
can be substituted for x that will makes the original equation a true statement. For this case, there are "no solutions."
