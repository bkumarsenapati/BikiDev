04.01H Honors

NOTE: Only complete this assignment if you are taking this course for Honors credit.

We use the properties you just learned to prove that a solution to an equation is correct.

  • Proofs are a very good example of deductive reasoning.
  • Deductive reasoning is the way a detective or an attorney puts together a case. He or she gathers the facts and puts them together in the proper order to prove a hypothesis.
  • Proofs are used in Algebra 1 & 2, Geometry, and Trigonometry.
  • In math, definitions, properties, axioms, and theorems can be used in a proof.
  • In algebra, we will use definitions, properties and axioms.

Let's get started.

Proofs have three parts: hypothesis, conclusion, and body.

The hypothesis is the beginning statement and the conclusion is the statement you are trying to prove.

The body is made up of the statements (steps in the proof) and the reasons for those steps. The definitions, properties, and axioms that you read about in the introduction above can be used as reasons in a proof.

Following are a few more properties added to the ones you just learned in assignment 04.02.


The Addition Property of Equality

If a, b, and c are real numbers and if a = b, then
a + c = b + c.

This means that the same quantity can be added to (or subtracted from) both sides of the equation without changing its value.

The Multiplication Property of Equality

If a, b, and c are real numbers and if a = b, then ac = bc.

This means that each side of the equation can be multiplied (or divided) by the same quantity without changing its value.

Substitution One number or variable can be substituted for another of the same value.
