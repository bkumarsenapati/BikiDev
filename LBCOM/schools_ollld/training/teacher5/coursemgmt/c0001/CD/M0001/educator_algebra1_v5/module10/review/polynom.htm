Multiplication of Polynomials
(Binomial x Binomial)

Multiplication can be more complicated than distributing one term. The example below demonstrates the multiplying of 2 binomials (2x - 7)(4x - 1). Go through the example and see if you can recognize a pattern. Afterwards, try the problems at the right. Let's look at some vocabulary.   Factors are the two expressions being multiplied: 2x - 7 and 4x - 1. The first terms of the factors are 2x and 4x because they come first if you are reading each expression individually. Outer terms are the terms physically on the outside of the two factors as they are written: 2x and -1. Inner terms are the terms physically on the inside of the two factors as they are written: -7 and 4x. Last terms are - 7 and - 1 because they come last if you are reading each expression individually. You multiply by double distribution or multiply first terms , outer terms , inner terms and then last terms. F O I L

If you look at the terms in the problem you will see FOIL. This is actually the same as distributing twice: first the 2x and second the -7.
F(first)
O(outer)
I(inner)
L(left)
(2x)(4x) (2x)(-1) (-7)(4x) (-7)(-1)
       
Make sure when multiplying coefficients are multiplied, exponents are added 8x2 - 2x - 28x + 7 Now is the time to add or subtract (combine like terms). -1x - 28x = -30x. Be careful not to add exponents, you are not multiplying but combining like terms.
8x2 - 30x + 7


These are the steps:
a. Multiply first terms
b. Multiply outside terms
c. Multiply inside terms
d. Multiply last terms
e. Collect like terms

Multiplication of Polynomials
(Polynomial x Polynomial)

Now, you have reached the pinnacle of multiplication! All multiplication is based on distributing and combining like terms. The trick is not to get lost in the process and knowing the rules for multiplication (add exponents) and the rules for addition/subtraction (do not change exponents). Below are some "large" multiplication problems done in other formats. (Notice the names!)

Linear Method
(All problems are written across in a straight line)

(2x)(x2) + (2x)(-5x) + (2x)(6) + (-1)(x2) + (-1)(-5x) + (-1)(6)

2x3 - 10x2 + 12x - x2 + 5x - 6

2x3 - 11x2 + 17x - 6

One note to help keep you straight. If you're multiplying 2 terms by 3 terms, before collecting like terms you should have (2)(3) = 6 terms in your answer. Check this before collecting terms, just in case you left one out. For a problem with 3 terms multiplied by 5 terms, you would have (3)(5) = 15 terms in your answer before you collect like terms.

Notice: the polynomials are usually written in descending order of their exponents. This is very important to the organization of the problem. It is more difficult to combine like terms if the terms are not in any order!
