05.06 Writing Equations of Lines Practice 3 Answers

In the lesson you were shown two ways to write the equation of the line given two points. You may choose to use either process. The explanations on this page are going to be shown using the point slope formula.

  1. To write the equation of a line, you must have the slope and a point on the line. We do not have the slope but we can find the slope from the two given points using the slope formula. The given points are (5,4) and (7,8).





    Now use the point slope formula to write the equation of the line. The slope is 2 and one of the points on the line is (5,4).



    y - 4 = 2(x - 5)

    y - 4 = 2x - 10

    y = 2x - 10 + 4

    y = 2x - 6
  1. To write the equation of a line, you must have the slope and a point on the line. We do not have the slope but we can find the slope from the two given points using the slope formula. The given points are (-2,-3) and (8,2).





    Now use the point slope formula to write the equation of the line. The slope is 1/2 and one of the points on the line is (8,2). (It does not matter which point you use in the formula, you will still get the same answer.)
  1. To write the equation of a line, you must have the slope and a point on the line. We do not have the slope but we can find the slope from the two given points using the slope formula. The given points are (1,0) and (0,5).





    Now use the point slope formula to write the equation of the line. The slope is -5 and one of the points on the line is (1,0).

    y - 0 = -5(x - 1)


    y = -5x + 5
