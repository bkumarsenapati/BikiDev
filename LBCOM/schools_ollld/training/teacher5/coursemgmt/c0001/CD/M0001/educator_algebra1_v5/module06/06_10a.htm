06.10 Pythagorean Theorem
Do you ever watch the home and garden channel? You are probably either an avid fan or have not even heard of it! The theorem you will learn in this lesson is often referred to on home and garden shows. The Pythagorean Theorem is a frequently applied Geometry theorem that allows you to both identify right angles and find missing sides of right triangles.
The Pythagorean Theorem

The Pythagorean Theorem states that in a right triangle, the sum of the square of each leg of the triangle is equal to the square of the hypotenuse.

Let's look at an illustration. In the picture below, the legs are represented by the letters A and B. The hypotenuse is always the side across from the right angle. In the picture below, the hypotenuse is represented by the letter C.

According to the theorem, you can square side A and add it to the square of side B to get the value of Hypotenuse C squared. Here is the algebraic equation associated with the theorem.

(Side)2 + (Side)2 = (Hypotenuse)2

OR

A2 + B2 = C2


How do we use the theorem?

Let's work first with the math skills involved in finding the missing part of a right triangle using the theorem.

Here are two examples.


Example 1

In the triangle below, use the Pythagorean Theorem to find the value of the missing side.

Remember the theorem is A2 + B2 = C2 where A and B are sides and C is the hypotenuse.

The missing side is represented by x and is the hypotenuse of the triangle (because it is across from the right angle). 10 and 24 are the values of the sides of the triangle. So, we can solve the following equation to find the value of x.

102 + 242 = x2
100 + 576 = x2
676 = x2


In order to find the value of x, you must calculate the square root of both sides of the equation.

The square root of 676 is 26 and the square root of x2 is x.

26 = x

The length of the hypotenuse of the triangle is 26 cm.

Example 2

In the triangle below, use the Pythagorean Theorem to find the value of the missing side.

Remember the theorem is A2 + B2 = C2 where A and B are sides and C is the hypotenuse.

The missing side is represented by x and is a side of the triangle. 3 is the value of the other side of the triangle and 5 is the value of the hypotenuse. So, we can solve the following equation to find the value of x.

x2 + 32 = 52
x2 + 9 = 25
x2 = 25 - 9
x2 = 16

In order to find the value of x, you must calculate the square root of both sides of the equation.

The square root of 676 is 26 and the square root of x2 is x.

x = 4

The length of the missing side of the triangle is 4 yd.

Now that you have some experience using the formula, you can begin to apply the formula to some real-life problems. In all the problems above, x was a whole number. Remember that life is not so neat. If you are working with real distances, you may find that some of your answers are in decimal form. Do not let this confuse you.

Let's look at some real-life examples.


Example 1

A 19-foot ladder just touches the bottom of a window. The bottom of the ladder is 9.25 feet from the base of the building. Which expression gives the height, in feet, from the ground to the bottom of the window?









Solution

Since the ladder, the wall of the building and the ground form a right angle, we can use the Pythagorean theorem to find the correct expression. The only tricky part is that rather than being asked to find the actual height, you are being asked to find the expression that would give you the height.

This means you have to think about "how" you would solve the problem. It is best to work towards solving the problem but leave your work in algebraic form.

The Pythagorean Theorem is (side)2 + (side)2 = (hypotenuse)2. Remember that the hypotenuse is across from the right angle. Let's use x for the missing side. The given values will fit into the formula to give you the following equation :

x2 + 9.252 = 192

Our goal is to solve for x. x2 = 192 - 9.252

Calculate the square root of both sides of the equation to find the value of x.

So, the answer is (1) .
