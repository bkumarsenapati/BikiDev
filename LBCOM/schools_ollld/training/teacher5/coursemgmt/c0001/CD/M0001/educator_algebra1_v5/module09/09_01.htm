09.01 Lab

Time for a little space math fun. We're going to take some time during this lab to learn about the solar system and to discover how large it really is.

Enjoy!

 

1. Diameter of the solar system is approximately: 7,500,000,000 miles

How long would it take to drive this distance? Assume you're going 60 miles per hour.

Example:

If I want to know how long it takes to travel 1000 miles at 60 miles per hour, I would use the formula: distance = rate x time

1000 = 60 x time

/60 /60

16.67 hours = time

2. The distance between the sun and Mercury is approximately: 36,000,000 miles

The distance between the sun and Venus is approximately 67,000,000 miles

The distance between the sun and Earth is approximately 93,000,000 miles

The distance between the sun and Mars is approximately 141,000,000 miles

The distance between the sun and Jupiter is approximately 484,000,000 miles

The distance between the sun and Saturn is approximately 887,000,000 miles

The distance between the sun and Uranus is approximately 1,800,000,000 miles

The distance between the sun and Neptune is approximately 2,800,000,000 miles

The distance between the sun and Pluto is approximately: 3,600,000,000 miles


Go to: http://janus.astro.umd.edu/astro/scinote and learn about scientific notation. Write each of the distances above in scientific notation.

3. Go to the website below (or one of your own choosing) and research facts about one of the nine planets listed above. Write a short paragraph in your own words about the planet you choose.

4. Post one interesting fact about the planet you researched in the student discussion area in the discussion group called "Planet Facts". Reply to at least one student's fact with a comment about their fact. Submit the interesting fact you posted and the interesting fact you read and replied to.
