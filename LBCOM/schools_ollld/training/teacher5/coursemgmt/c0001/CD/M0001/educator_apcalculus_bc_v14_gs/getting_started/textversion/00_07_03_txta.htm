What is Plagiarism

What is plagiarism, and how can I avoid it?

The original material:

Logic will get you from A to B. Imagination will take you everywhere.

Source: The Ultimate Quotable Einstein by Alice Calaprice and Freeman Dyson, p. 481

Examples of incorrect citations

Logic will get you from A to B. Imagination will take you everywhere.
Not citing a source implies that these words are yours. It's dishonest and definitely plagiarism.

I think that logic can only take you so far. Imagination, however, can take you anywhere.
Paraphrasing other's words as your own opinion is plagiarism.

Logic will get you pretty far, but imagination can take you everywhere.
It doesn't matter that you changed a few words. Paraphrasing someone else's words without credit is plagiarism.

According to Albert Einstein, imagination can take you farther than a jet plane.
Misrepresenting a source's meaning is dishonest—don't do it!

According to Albert Einstein, imagination can take you a lot further than logic.
This paraphrase is better, but it needs to include a link to the source so readers can interpret the original quotation for themselves.

"Logic will get you from A to B," said Albert Einstein. "Imagination will take you everywhere."
Although this quote credits Albert Einstein, it doesn't credit the source that reported his words. Almost there!

Examples of correct citations

A good imagination is essential for awesome daydreams.
Since this quote neither resembles Albert Einstein’s nor implies these words are his, you’re good to go.

“Logic will get you from A to B,” said Albert Einstein. “Imagination will take you everywhere.”
(Source: The Ultimate Quotable Einstein by Alice Calaprice and Freeman Dyson, p. 481)
Perfect – no plagiarism here! Note the use of quotation marks, inline citation, and reference to the original source.
