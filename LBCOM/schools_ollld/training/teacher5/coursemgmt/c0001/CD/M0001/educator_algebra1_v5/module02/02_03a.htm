02.03 Charts

Stem and Leaf chart

The SAT scores for a group of 20 seniors is shown in the chart below. The numbers in the stem column are the hundreds place digits and the numbers in the leaf column are the ones place digits. So the scores represented in the first row are 886, 895, and 898. You simply combine the digits to get the actual scores. The second row of scores are 925, 925, 955, and 990.


To find the mean of the data, add all the scores together and divide by 20. This is often referred to as the average.

 

To find the median of the data, list the scores in order from smallest to largest and find the number that is in the middle. If there are two numbers in the middle, find the average of them.

 

To find the mode of the data, locate the score(s) that appear the most often. There may be more than one mode.
