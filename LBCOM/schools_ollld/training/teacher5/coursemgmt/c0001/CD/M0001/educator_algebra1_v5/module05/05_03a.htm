05.03 Advanced Slope

What we already know about SLOPE
If you were skateboarding up or down ramps, the steepness of the ramp would determine the difficulty of your run. The steeper the incline or decline, the more experienced you would need to be. In math, we call the steepness of the incline or decline of a line, the SLOPE.

 

 

Slope tells us the steepness of the line. Slope compares how fast a line goes up (incline) and down (decline) with how fast the line goes right and left. The bigger the slope, the steeper the line. The smaller the slope, the flatter the line.

In this lesson you will learn how to find the slope of a line three ways (you already know one way).


Method #1: Find the slope of a line if you know the equation of the line

Example: y = 2x - 3
Here m = 2, so our slope is 2.

We know that we find the slope by looking at a linear equation in the form
y = mx + b. (m is the slope of the line). So, method 1 is not a problem if we have an equation in slope intercept form.

What if we want to know the slope of a line and we do not know the equation? No problem! This is the challenge for this lesson.


Method # 2: The slope can be found if we have the graph of the line and can find the coordinates of two points that are on the line. Remember, two points on the line are really two solution sets for the equation.

Given any two points on a line, we can think of slope as the amount the y coordinates change in relation to the amount the x coordinates change.

We call the change of y the RISE (because the y values go in the vertical direction, UP or DOWN) and the change of x the RUN (because the x values go in the horizontal direction or ACROSS)

Summary:

Slope is the amount of incline or decline of a line.
Three ways to find the slope are:

a. Look at an equation in slope intercept form and locate the m

b. Look at the graph of a line, find two points, find the rise and run, and put in a fraction form as rise over run.

c. Use the slope formula when two points are known.
