03.07 Writing and Evaluating Basic Algebraic Expressions

We will now look at writing and evaluating some basic algebraic expressions. This is an important skill that you will be using throughout the rest of your travels.

Travel Advisory: A number next to a letter or variable means you multiply. 3y is 3 times the value of y.

We have learned that in an algebraic expression letters can stand for numbers. When we substitute a specific value for each variable and then perform the operations, it is called evaluating expressions.

Evaluate the expression 3x - y
Let x = 7 and y = -11

This means wherever you see x put the number 7 and wherever you see y put the number -11.
You "substitute"  7 in place of x and you substitute -11 in place of y

Here is what your expression now looks like:

3(7) - (-11) =
21 - (-11) =
21 + 11 = 32

Evaluate the expression 3x - y
Let x = 4 and y = 12

If you change the value of your variables, your answer changes even though the expression stays the same.

Here is the new expression

3(4) - 12 =
12 - 12 = 0


This information is very important and is the formation of algebra. You will find questions about this on the upcoming quiz.
