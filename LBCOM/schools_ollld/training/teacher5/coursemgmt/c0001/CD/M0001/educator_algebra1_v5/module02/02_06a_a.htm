02.06 Ratios

Ratios can tell us a lot of useful information we use to make decisions.

A Ratio is a comparison of two or more quantities.

  • Order is key when writing ratios
  • Ratios can be written as fractions
  • Ratios can be written using ratio symbols 2 to 4 or 2:4

Let's look at some examples:

Order is key

You are going to a dance at the school this weekend. Your teacher says that the ratio of girls to boys at the dance will be 8:3 or . What does this mean?

You can expect that for every eight girls, there will be three boys. The ratio helps us make a comparison. The ratio is girls (first in the ratio) compared to boys (second in the ratio). Therefore, the notation indicates girls first (the 8) and boys next (the 3).

If you are coming to meet that special someone, your chances are better if you are a boy since there will be many more girls at the dance!


What else can ratios do for us?

Let's take this to the next step. Suppose I know that the ratio of girls to boys will be 8:3. I know that 15 boys will attend. How many girls will be there?

Set up a proportion

Cross multiply: (8)(15) = (3)x
120 = 3x
Solve for x so Divide by 120 by 3; x= 40

There will be 40 girls at the dance.
This makes sense because the ratio 8:3 says that there will just less than 3x the number of girls than boys. 40 is a bit less than 45 boys.
