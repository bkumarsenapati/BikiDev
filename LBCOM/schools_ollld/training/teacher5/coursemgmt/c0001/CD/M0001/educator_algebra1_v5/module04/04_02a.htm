04.02 Simplify

Getting together to complete a task is a fact of life. In order to solve problems (algebra or otherwise), the act of simplifying before solving must be accomplished.

Before you can begin simplifying, you need to be familiar with some algebraic vocabulary words and phrases. Listed below are those words and phrases:

  1. Coefficient - A number written in front of a variable.
       Example: the number 2 in the term 2x.
  2. Constant - A term that contains only numbers, no variables.
       Example: the term 5 in the expression 2x + 5.
  3. Term - Variables, numbers, or variables and numbers that are separated by addition or subtraction signs.
       Example: 3x + 5 contains two terms. 3x is a term and +5 is also a term.
  4. Like Terms - Terms that contain identical variable parts.
       Example: 3x and 2x are like terms. 4x and 5xy are unlike terms.
  5. Expression - A mathematical phrase that represents a value.
       Example: 3x + 5 is an expression.
    (Be aware that sometimes students confuse the words expression and equation. An expression is not a complete mathematical sentence. We can simplify an expression, but we cannot solve for a missing variable. An equation always contains an equal sign and is a complete mathematical sentence. You can solve an equation to find a missing variable.)

Simplifying using the Distributive property

Distributing is used to eliminate parentheses from a problem. When simplifying expressions and solving equations, distributing should be your first step.

Distributing involves multiplication.
When distributing, you will multiply the term outside the parentheses with each term within the parentheses.

Let's look at a simple example.

Example 1
6(3x + 7) Simplify the expression by using the distributive property.
= 6(3x) + 6(7)

Multiply the 6 with the 3x and then with the 7.

=18x + 42 Simplify and write the answer to the expression.

You should pay particular attention to the signs when you distribute. In general a minus sign is the same as a negative and a plus sign is the same as a positive.

Remember...

  • A negative times a negative is a positive.
  • A negative times a positive is a negative.

Let's look at a couple of examples involving integers.

Example 2
-2(m - 8) Simplify the expression by using the distributive property.
= -2(m) -2(-8)

Multiply the -2 with the m and then with the -8.

= -2m + 16 Simplify and write the answer to the expression.

Example 3
-5(2x + 3) Simplify the expression by using the distributive property.
=-5(2x) -5(3)

Multiply the -5 with the 2x and then with the 3.

= -10x -15 Simplify and write the answer to the expression.

Simplifying by collecting like terms
Collecting like terms is used to simplify expressions. You should collect like terms after you distribute.

Collecting like terms involves addition.
When collecting like terms you will add the coefficients of the terms that have the exact same variables.

You should pay particular attention to the signs when you collect like terms.

Remember...

  • When adding numbers with the same sign, add the numbers and keep the sign.
  • When adding numbers with different signs, subtract the numbers and keep the sign of the number with the largest absolute value.

Let's look at some examples.
Example 4
2x - 3y - 6x - 8y Simplify the expression by combining like terms.
= 2x - 6x - 3y - 8y

Rearrange the terms so that the like terms are together. Make sure you move the negative sign with the 3y term.
= -4x -11y Combine the like terms by adding their coefficients and keeping the variables.

Example 5
5ab - ab -6 + 3 Simplify the expression by combining like terms.
= 5ab - 1ab -6 + 3

HINT: Consider the -ab as -1ab.

= 4ab - 3 Combine the like terms by adding their coefficients and keeping the variables.

Putting it together - It's time to execute

Now that you can distribute and collect like terms, it is time to do both operations within one problem. Remember that you should distribute first and then collect like terms.

Here are a few examples. Watch those signs!


Example 6
5 + 3(4x - 7) Simplify the expression by combining like terms.
= 5 + 12x - 21

Step 1: Distribute the 3 times the 4x and then times the 7.
= 12x - 16

Step 2: Collect like terms (5 and -21)

Usually, you will write your answer with the variable term before the constant term.


Example 7
2(z - 5) + (z - 6) Simplify
= 2z - 10 + z - 6

Step 1: Distribute 2 times z and 2 times -5 in the first part of the problem. Hint: The second set of parentheses does not appear to have a factor in front of it. The number you should distribute is 1.

= 3z - 16

Step 2: Collect like terms (2z and z are like terms and the -10 and -6 are like terms.)

Example 8
4(3x + 1) - 8(x - 4) Simplify
= 12x + 4 - 8x + 32

Step 1: Distribute 4 times 3x and 4 times 1 in the first part of the problem. In the second part of the problem, distribute -8 times x and -8 times -4. (Please note that -8 times -4 makes +32.)

= 4x + 36

Step 2: Collect like terms (12x and -8x are like terms and the 4 and 32 are like terms)

Example 9
-10n -5(3n + 4) Simplify
= -10n - 15n - 20

Step 1: Distribute the -5 times the 3n and times the +4. Warning, the 5 is negative and will multiply with the +4 to make - 20.

= -25n - 20

Step 2: Collect like terms (-10n and -15n are like terms)
