07.07 Factoring Completely

Factoring Completely

In this lesson, we will review all the methods for factoring and use them to factor all types of polynomials. Now, you should recognize everything on this chart and know what it means. You should start every factoring problem by working it through this chart. If you do, you can factor almost anything.

Step 1
Factor out the Greatest Common Factor
Step 2
Determine which factoring method to use based on the number of terms

Binomial

Is it a difference of squares?

a2 - b2 = (a + b)(a - b)

Trinomial

Does it look like x2 + bx + c

or like ax2 + bx + c?

4-Term Polynomial

Can you factor it by grouping?

 

Step 3
Check to see that nothing can be factored further.

General Factoring Examples

Please take the time to work through all of these examples carefully. There are many of them, but each one was written for your benefit.


 Example 1: Factor 10x3 - 40x
   10x3 - 40x

= 10x(x2 - 4)		 Step 1: Look for a GCF. If there is one, factor it out.
= 10x(x + 2)(x - 2) Step 2: Determine which factoring method to use on the parentheses part. Since there are two terms inside the parenthesis, let's try difference of squares.
10x(x + 2)(x - 2) is our final answer.

Step 3: Can any of the parentheses be factored further? Here is what you should check.

  • Is there a GCF in any of the parentheses?
  • Are any of the variables raised to a power other than 1?
  • Is there a difference of squares in any of the parentheses?

Since we cannot answer yes to any of these, our problem cannot be factored any further.

Example 2: Factor y5 - 2y4 - 35y3
   y5 - 2y4 - 35y3

= y3(y2 - 2y - 35)		 Step 1: Look for a GCF. If there is one, factor it out.
= y3(y + 5)(y - 7) Step 2: Determine which factoring method to use. Since there are three terms inside the parenthesis and the coefficient of y2 is 1, we can factor by guess and check. (What numbers multiply to -35 and add up to the middle term, -2?)
y3(y + 5)(y - 7) is our final answer.

Step 3: Can any of the parentheses be factored further? Here is what you should check.

  • Is there a GCF in any of the parentheses?
  • Are any of the variables raised to a power other than 1?
  • Is there a difference of squares in any of the parentheses?

Since we cannot answer yes to any of these, our problem cannot be factored any further.

Example 3: Factor 2x2 - 11x + 12
  2x2 - 11x + 12
 Step 1: Look for a GCF. If there is one, factor it out. (There is no GCF here.)

2(12) = 24

    2x2 -11x+ 12

= 2x2 -8x - 3x + 12

= 2x(x - 4)    -3(x - 4)

= (x - 4)(2x - 3)

 

Step 2: Determine which factoring method to use. Since there are three terms in the polynomial and there is a coefficient on the first term, we can factor by grouping.

  1. Multiply the first and last coefficients.
  2. List all the factor pairs for the number + 24.
  3. Find the pair of factors that adds to the middle coefficient, -11.
  4. Rewrite the expression using your two new factors (3 and -8) to replace the middle term.
  5. Factor out the GCF for each half of the polynomial.
  6. If the parentheses match, factor it out as a GCF for the entire problem.
(x - 4)(2x - 3) is our final answer.

Step 3: Can any of the parentheses be factored further? Here is what you should check.

  • Is there a GCF in any of the parentheses?
  • Are any of the variables raised to a power other than 1?
  • Is there a difference of squares in any of the parentheses?

Since we cannot answer yes to any of these, our problem cannot be factored any further.

Example 4: Factor 3m4 - 3
   3m4 - 3

= 3(m4 - 1) 		Step 1: Look for a GCF. If there is one, factor it out.
= 3(m2 + 1)(m2 - 1) Step 2: Determine which factoring method to use on the parentheses part. Since there are two terms inside the parenthesis, let's try difference of squares.

= 3(m2 + 1)(m2 - 1)

= 3(m2 + 1)(m + 1)(m - 1)

Step 3: Can any of the parentheses be factored further? Here is what you should check.

  • Is there a GCF in any of the parentheses?
  • Are any of the variables raised to a power other than 1?
  • Is there a difference of squares in any of the parentheses?

The last parentheses contains another difference of squares. Factor it.


Example 5: Factor 2x4 - 5x2 - 12
   2x4 - 5x2 - 12 Step 1: Look for a GCF. If there is one, factor it out. (There is no GCF here.)

2(-12) = -24

   2x4 - 5x2 - 12

= 2x4 - 8x2 + 3x2 - 12

= 2x2(x2 - 4)   + 3(x2 - 4)

= (x2 - 4)(2x2 + 3)

Step 2: Determine which factoring method to use. Since there are three terms in the polynomial and there is a coefficient on the first term, we can factor by grouping.

  1. Multiply the first and last coefficients.
  2. List all the factor pairs for the number - 24.
  3. Find the pair of factors that adds to the middle coefficient, -5.
  4. Use 3 and -8 to write the trinomial as a 4-term polynomial.
  5. Now group each pair of terms and factor each group.
  6. If the parentheses match, factor it out as a GCF for the entire problem.

= (x2 - 4)(2x2 + 3)

= (x + 2)(x - 2) (2x2 + 3)

Step 3: Can any of the parentheses be factored further? Here is what you should check.

  • Is there a GCF in any of the parentheses?
  • Are any of the variables raised to a power other than 1?
  • Is there a difference of squares in any of the parentheses?

The first parentheses contains another difference of squares. Factor it.
