02.02 Graphs Assignment

We will now take some time to look at how graphs can provide us with some extremely useful information.

  • Visit the National Center for Educational Statistics (NCES) classroom. Click on "Create a Graph."
  • View the EXAMPLES (click on the examples tab) to look at each of the items listed below. You do not actually need to create a graph.

---------- Select the Examples ---------
US Public School Student Membership
Percentage of Students who Reported Being Bullied at School
Airline On-Time Statistics and Delay Causes

Use the information needed from the National Center for Educational Statistics and from this lesson to answer the questions. You will be checking your answers at the end. Make sure to complete this assignment because you will be tested on this information.

Questions

1. Using the data from the US Public School Student Membership

a. What type of graph is shown?
b. Make a statement about the school membership from 1990 to 2000.

2. Using the data from the Percentage of Students who Reported Being Bullied at School

a. What type of graph is shown?
b. What does the data tell us about the percent of students reporting begin bullied by grade level?
c. What does the data tell us about the percent of students being bullied from 1999 to 2003?

3. Using the data from Airline On-Time Statistics and Delay Causes

a. What type of graph is shown?
b. What is the major reason for airline delays? How do you know?
c. Make a statement about the relationship between airline delays and equipment.
