Multiplying and Dividing Integers
  1. When the signs are the same: Add the numbers and keep the sign.

    Examples:
    • -2 + -4 = -6
    • 8 + 3 = 11

  2. When the signs are different: Subtract the numbers and keep the sign of the larger number.

    Examples:
    • 5 + -8 = -3
    • -6 + 7 =
    • -12 + 15 = 3

Before you do any math, you must change the subtraction sign to addition and change the sign of the second number.
After make the changes, use the addition rules.

Examples:

  • -4 - 5 becomes -4 + -5 which equals -9.
  • 3 - (-2) becomes 3 + 2 which equals 5.
  • 12 - 16 becomes 12 + -16 which equals -4
  • -8 - (-5) becomes -8 + 5 which equals -3

An even number of negative signs is positive. An odd number of negative signs is negative.

Even: (2 negatives, 4 negatives, etc.)

  • -3 * -4 = 12
  • -3 * -3 = 9
  • -6* -2 *-1 * -3 = 36
  • -20/-4 = 5 (This is division)

Odd: (1 negative, 3 negatives, etc.)

  • -3 * 5 = -15
  • -2 * -4 * -2 = -16
  • 12/-3 = -4

Lesson 3.5
English to Algebra

How do you write "less than a number"?

Consecutive even and odd integers

8 less than a number
n - 8

3 less than twice a number
2n - 3

Three consecutive odd integers would be like 15, 17 and 19.

Three consecutive even integers would be like 10, 12 and 14

Both consecutive even numbers and consecutive odd numbers are written algebraically as follows:

x = the first integer
x + 2 = the second integer
x + 4 = the third integer

So if the sum of three consecutive odd integers is 21, you write:
x + (x + 2) + (x + 4) = 21
