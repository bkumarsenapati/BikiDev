Factoring

Here is your review of factoring by grouping. This is the only method you must know. If you remember the shortcuts, feel free to use them when appropriate.

Work the 8 problems at the bottom of this review.

Background:
Examine the following problems and see if you recognize a pattern.

(2x + 1)(3x + 2) = 6x2 + 7x + 2
(3x - 2)(4x + 1) = 12x2 - 5x - 2
(x - 4)(2x - 5) = 2x2 -13x + 20

The first and last terms are found by looking for two numbers whose product is a in the quadratic equation, but the middle term is a little more complicated. The best way to see if you do these problems correctly is to always take your answer and FOIL it back out to see if you factored correctly.

When factoring trinomials, you are essentially working backwards from the FOIL method. There is a process which (if the expression is not prime) will always allow you to factor a quadratic equation. At first, the process will seem cumbersome, but once you have it, you will never be lost when factoring.

 
Let's try one of the equations above, since we have the answer already, we'll know when it's right:

Step 1: Find the product of the quadratic term (the a in the general form) and the constant term (that's the c).

Step 2: Find two numbers which have the result of step 1 as their product, and the linear term (that's the b) as their sum.
In this example, we want two numbers that multiply to give 12 and add to give 7... 3 and 4 work!

Step 3: Rewrite the original expression, but instead of the original linear term, use the sum of the two numbers found in step 2.

Step 4: Group the first two terms and the last two terms and find a GCF for each group.

Notice by removing both GCF's the quantity left is the same, if an expression is factorable, this will always happen, although you must be careful with the signs, especially if the third term is negative.

Step 5: Now, find the GCF of the whole expression. There are now only two terms.
3x(2x+1) + 2(2x+1) isn't much different from 3xy + 2y, where y = (2x +1). If you just had the y, you would easily factor it out : y(3x + 2).You can do the same thing here.

Factor: 6x2 + 7x + 2


Step 1:6(2) = 12


Step 2: 3(4) = 12; 3+4 = 7


Step 3: 
6x2 + 7x + 2 = 6x2 + 3x + 4x + 2

Step 4:
(6x2 + 3x )+ (4x + 2) =
3x(2x +1) + 2(2x +1)


Step 5:
3x(2x+1) + 2(2x+1)=
(2x+1)(3x +2)

Check by FOIL
(2x+1)(3x +2) =6x2 + 7x + 2

4. 4x2 - 25
Check for a GCF! -none

Step 1:4(-25) = -100

Step 2: 10(-10) = -100; 10+ -10 = 0

Step 3: 4x2 - 25 = 4x2+ 10x - 10x - 25

Step 4: (4x2+ 10x)- (10x + 25) =
2x(2x + 5) - 5(2x +5)
Be careful of the signs. Notice when the last two terms were grouped, the subtraction sign was left outside. That is just like factoring out a -1. So, inside the parentheses, the 10x and the 25 are now positive!

Step 5: 2x(2x + 5) - 5(2x +5)= (2x+5)(2x - 5)

FOIL TO CHECK!
