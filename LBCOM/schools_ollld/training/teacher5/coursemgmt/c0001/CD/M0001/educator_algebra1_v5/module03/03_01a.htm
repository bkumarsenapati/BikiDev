03.01 Number System Relationships

Now it's time to take a look at some specific types of numbers that are commonly used in Algebra. You will learn about number systems in two ways; first using a table and then a picture.

Real Number System

All the numbers that are described below are REAL numbers.

Rational Numbers

Positive and negative fractions, decimals that terminate, and whole numbers.

Examples of rational numbers:
1, -2, 0, 1/2, 2.5,

 

Irrational Numbers

Decimals that do not terminate (end) or repeat.
Usually irrational numbers are in the form of square roots.

Examples of irrational numbers:
1.2335487698574... (never ending)

The following sets are all part of the set of Rational Numbers:

Integers are positive and negative whole numbers and zero {...,-2,-1,0,1,2,.....}

Whole numbers {0,1,2,3,......}

Counting numbers {1,2,3,4,....} It is important to understand that these number groups are subsets of each other. For example, a number like 5 would be a counting number but it would also be a whole number and an integer. A number like -4 would only be an integer because it is negative and therefore could not fit into the whole number or counting number group. ALL of these numbers are rational and real.
