05.02 Slope and Intercepts Practice

Using our Intercept knowledge

1. Given the equation 2y = 4x - 6, what are the coordinates of the y intercept and the x intercept.

Answer: If this equation was in slope intercept form, we could simply find the b value and we would know the y intercept. However, it is not in that form. BUT..we do know that x=0 at the y intercept, so substitute 0 for x and solve for y.

2y = 4(0) -6
2y = -6 [divide by 2 to solve for y]
y = -3 Therefore the coordinates of the y intercept are (0, -3)

Use the same reasoning to find the x intercept. y=0 at the x intercept. Therefore, substitute y=0 in the equation and solve for x in the equation y = 4x-6.

2(0) = 4x -6
0 = 4x-6 [add 6 to each side of the equation]

6 = 4x [divide by 4 to get x]
= x Simplify and you get = x

The coordinates of the x intercept are ( , 0)
