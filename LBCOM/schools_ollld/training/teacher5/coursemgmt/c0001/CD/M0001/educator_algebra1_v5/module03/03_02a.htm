03.02 Integers

Integers include positive and negative whole numbers and zero.

Examples

-104, -12, 0, 4, 36

Non-examples

1/3, 6.00036, 2.5, and -3.1428571

What are some real life examples of integers you ask?
  • Well, if you've ever lived in Wisconsin, you know that in the winter the temperature can get below zero or negative...(brrrrr)
  • In football, a player can gain positive or negative yards depending on which way he runs.
  • In banking, if you spend more money than you have in your account, you're said to be in the red or negative, however, if you win the lottery that would be a positive deposit.
  • The stock market has positive and negative gains/losses every day!
Okay, so now that I know what an integer is, how do I use them?

The first thing you must remember is that the rules for adding and subtracting integers are different than the rules for multiplying and dividing integers.
Please don't forget that as you journey on.

Let's start with addition and subtraction
Rules for Adding Integers
  1. When the SIGNS are the SAME:
     Add the numbers and keep the sign.

Examples:
-2 + -4 = -6
8 + 3 = 11

  1. When the SIGNS are DIFFERENT:
    Subtract the numbers and keep the sign of the larger number.

Examples:
5 + -8 = -3
-6 + 7 = 1
-12 + 15 = 3


Rules for Subtracting Integers

Before you do any math, you must change the subtraction sign to addition and change the sign of the second number.
After you make the sign changes, use the addition rules.

Examples:
-4 - 5 becomes -4 + -5 which equals -9.
3 - (-2) becomes 3 + 2 which equals 5.
12 - 16 becomes 12 + -16 which equals -4
-8 - (-5) becomes -8 + 5 which equals -3


Let's move on to multiplication and division
Multiplying and Dividing Integers

An even number of negative signs is positive. An odd number of negative signs is negative.

Even: (2 negatives, 4 negatives, etc.)
-3(-4) = 12
-3(-3) = 9
-6(-2)(-1)(-3) = 36
-20/-4=5 (This is division)

Odd: (1 negative, 3 negatives, etc.)
-3(5) = -15
-2(-4)(-2) = -16
12/-3 = -4 (This is division)
