The first way most people learn to graph is by using the three points method. This is the most time consuming way, but it always works and requires the least amount of algebraic knowledge. The method takes less time if the equation is first solved for y. Look at the example below. The second way to graph is by finding the x and y intercepts. This is quick and easy once you learn it, but it does not work for equations in only one variable (x = 3). We will talk about those later. To graph by intercepts, the equation should be in standard form. Then substitute 0 for x, solve for y to find the y-intercept. Substitute 0 for y and solve for x to find the x-intercept. The third way to graph is the quickest, and takes the most algebraic knowledge. The equation must be in slope-intercept form, but no further solving is necessary.
